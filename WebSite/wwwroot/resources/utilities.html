Utilities

Service providers for Goswick Ranch and the Mayer area.

Water — Private Wells

Goswick Ranch lots are served by individual private wells, not the Mayer Domestic Water Improvement District (MDWID). MDWID serves the town of Mayer and portions of Poland Junction; confirm with the HOA board regarding any shared well arrangements on the property.

Exempt domestic wells (≤35 gallons per minute) in Arizona do not require a water right permit. However, they do require a Notice of Intent to Drill through the Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR).

Arsenic Warning — Test Your Well

The Big Bug Mining District geology produces elevated arsenic in groundwater. Research shows that 43.6% of water wells sampled in Yavapai County exceeded the EPA's 10 parts-per-billion (ppb) drinking water standard for arsenic. Arsenic is odorless, colorless, and tasteless — you cannot detect it without testing.

The Arizona Department of Environmental Quality recommends testing private wells every 5 years for arsenic and other mining-related contaminants. Contact a certified water testing laboratory or your county extension office for sampling kits.

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is legal in Arizona and can supplement well water for outdoor irrigation. The Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR) offers reimbursement grants of up to $2,000 for residential rainwater harvesting systems with tanks of 800 gallons or more. Contact ADWR for current program availability and application details.

Important — Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS): APS has identified the Mayer area for potential Public Safety Power Shutoffs during high wildfire risk conditions (extreme heat, low humidity, high winds). Residents on private wells should maintain a generator capable of running their well pump — a minimum of 3,000 watts is recommended for most residential well systems. See Emergency Services for PSPS preparedness details.

Solar Energy

At 5,000+ feet elevation with 300+ sunny days per year, Goswick Ranch properties are well-suited for solar. Key incentives as of early 2026:

  • The federal 30% Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for solar was eliminated effective January 1, 2026. Homeowners who installed solar before that date locked in the credit.
  • Arizona's 25% state income tax credit (maximum $1,000 per system) remains in effect.
  • APS net metering: APS pays approximately $0.062 per kWh for exported solar generation, locked for 10 years under current tariffs.

Consult a licensed solar contractor for current program details. Any solar installation visible from neighboring lots may require ARC approval — check with the board before proceeding.

Propane

Natural gas pipelines do not extend to the Goswick Ranch area. Most homes use propane for heating, cooking, and water heating. Propane is delivered by truck and stored in tanks on your property.

Confirmed local propane providers with documented Mayer-area service:

  • Ferrellgas — Chino Valley location: (928) 445-3940
  • John Graves Propane — explicitly serves the Mayer area; call for current rates and will-call vs. automatic delivery options

Propane prices fluctuate seasonally. Typical range in rural Yavapai County is $1.50–$2.00 per gallon for will-call delivery; automatic delivery contracts may offer different pricing. Maintain a minimum 20% tank level to avoid runout during winter weather or wildfire evacuations.

Internet & Cellular

Cellular coverage: All three major carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) cover approximately 73–77% of the Mayer 86333 zip code area. On-site coverage at Goswick Ranch may vary due to terrain — the ridgeline and elevation affect signal propagation. A cellular signal booster may improve indoor reception.

Starlink (SpaceX satellite internet) is confirmed available in ZIP code 86333 and is the most practical high-speed option for properties on Goswick Ranch:

  • Speeds: 50–180 Mbps download / 30–50 ms latency (typical rural residential)
  • Monthly cost: $80–$120 for the Residential plan
  • Equipment: ~$349 one-time hardware cost (Starlink dish + router)
  • No data caps on residential plan
  • Service available immediately — no waitlist for ZIP 86333
  • Website: starlink.com

Other options for the area:

  • CenturyLink / Lumen — DSL service available in parts of Mayer; speeds vary widely by distance from hub
  • HughesNet / Viasat — geostationary satellite; higher latency (600+ ms) than Starlink; suitable for basic web browsing
  • HAM radio — Mt. Union repeater (147.260 MHz) and a GMRS repeater (462.600 MHz) provide good local radio coverage for licensed operators
