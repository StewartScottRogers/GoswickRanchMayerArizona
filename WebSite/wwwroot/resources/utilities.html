Utilities

Service providers for Goswick Ranch and the Mayer area.

Water — Private Wells

Goswick Ranch lots are served by individual private wells — there is no community water system. Each lot is fully responsible for its own water supply. The Mayer Domestic Water Improvement District (MDWID) serves the Mayer townsite and portions of Poland Junction, but not the Goswick Ranch subdivision.

In the fractured granite terrain of the Bradshaws, wells are found in fracture zones — typical productive depths are 130–525 feet below surface. Rapid drainage in granite means wells may respond quickly to drought conditions.

Exempt domestic wells (≤35 gallons per minute) do not require a water right permit in Arizona. They do require a Notice of Intent to Drill filed with the Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR): azwater.gov. Look up specific wells by parcel: ADWR Well Registry.

Arsenic Warning — Test Your Well

The Big Bug Mining District geology produces elevated arsenic in groundwater. Research shows that 43.6% of water wells sampled in Yavapai County exceeded the EPA's 10 parts-per-billion (ppb) drinking water standard for arsenic. The Henrietta Mine, which lies within or immediately adjacent to the subdivision, is a documented source of acid mine drainage and metal leaching risk.

Arsenic is odorless, colorless, and tasteless — you cannot detect it without testing. Test every 5 years for arsenic, lead, nitrates, and mining-related metals. Point-of-use reverse osmosis systems are effective and widely available.

ADHS well water testing resources: azdhs.gov/wellwater — free testing has been available through ADHS/UA Cooperative Extension grants (check current availability).

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting is legal in Arizona with no tank size limit for residential systems used for outdoor irrigation or non-potable indoor use (no permit required). The Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR) offers reimbursement grants of up to $2,000 for residential systems with tanks of 800 gallons or more ($1.00/gallon reimbursement). At ~14–16 inches annual precipitation and a 2,500 sq ft roof, a Goswick Ranch property could yield approximately 15,000–20,000 gallons/year — useful for livestock, irrigation, and emergency storage. ADWR Grants: water.az.gov/rural-programs/grants

Solar Energy

At 5,000–6,000 feet elevation with 300+ sunny days per year, Goswick Ranch properties are among the best solar locations in Arizona. Key incentives as of early 2026:

  • The federal 30% Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for residential solar was eliminated effective January 1, 2026 (signed July 4, 2025). Homeowners who installed solar before that date locked in the credit.
  • Arizona's 25% state income tax credit (maximum $1,000 per system) remains in effect with no current end date.
  • Arizona property tax exemption: solar-added value is not assessed for property taxes.
  • Arizona sales tax exemption: 5.6% savings on solar equipment purchase.
  • APS net billing: APS pays approximately $0.0617 per kWh for exported solar generation, locked for 10 years from interconnection. Battery storage is increasingly attractive to store excess solar at full retail value (~$0.15–$0.25/kWh).

Consult a licensed solar contractor for current program details. Any solar installation visible from neighboring lots may require ARC approval — check with the board before proceeding. Yavapai County building permits are required.

Propane

Natural gas pipelines do not extend to the Goswick Ranch area. Most homes use propane for heating, cooking, and water heating. Propane is delivered by truck and stored in on-site tanks.

Confirmed propane providers with documented Mayer-area service:

  • Ferrellgas — 3225 N State Route 89, Chino Valley, AZ 86323 · (928) 445-3940 · Explicitly lists Mayer in service area; Mon–Fri 8am–5pm; 24/7 emergency line
  • AmeriGas — 1-800-263-7442 · Service confirmed for Mayer area; use location finder at amerigas.com
  • John Graves Propane — (928) 567-2425 (Camp Verde) · Explicitly lists Mayer, Prescott Valley, Dewey in service area; serves much of Northern Arizona
  • Gilligan's Spring Valley 76 — 17257 E Spring Valley Rd · (928) 632-4139 · AmeriGas dealer on-site; convenient local option

Propane prices fluctuate seasonally. Typical range in rural Yavapai County is $1.50–$2.00/gallon for will-call delivery; automatic delivery contracts may offer different pricing. Maintain a minimum 20% tank level to avoid runout during winter weather or wildfire evacuations.

Note for gated community: Confirm with your chosen propane supplier that their delivery trucks can access gated subdivisions — drivers will need gate access arrangements.

Starlink (SpaceX satellite internet) is confirmed available in ZIP code 86333 and is the most practical high-speed option for Goswick Ranch properties:

  • Download speeds: 50–180 Mbps · Upload: 10–25 Mbps · Latency: 20–60ms
  • Monthly cost: Residential Lite ~$80/mo; Residential MAX ~$120/mo
  • Equipment: ~$349 one-time hardware cost (Standard Kit) or ~$249 (Mini) + shipping
  • No data caps on residential plan; no waitlist for ZIP 86333
  • Works well with clear southern sky view — no obstruction issues at 6,000 ft with open sightlines
  • Assessment: Fully capable for professional remote work
  • Website: starlink.com

Other options:

  • CenturyLink / Lumen — DSL service available in parts of Mayer; speeds vary widely by distance from hub
  • HughesNet / Viasat — Geostationary satellite; higher latency (600+ ms) than Starlink; suitable for basic web browsing
  • HAM radio — Mt. Union repeater and GMRS repeater provide local radio coverage for licensed operators; useful for emergency communications
