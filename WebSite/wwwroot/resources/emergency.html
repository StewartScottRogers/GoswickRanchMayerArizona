Mayer Fire District

The Mayer Fire District serves Goswick Ranch for fire, medical, rescue, and advanced life support (ALS) emergencies. It is a combination district (paid and volunteer) with three stations (Station 21, 22, and 23). The primary service area covers approximately 8,000 residents across 52 square miles; extended response coverage reaches an additional 2,000 people across roughly 302 square miles total. The district also provides first-responder coverage to Prescott National Forest, BLM lands, state lands, and Agua Fria National Monument.

Response times vary based on terrain and road conditions — residents are encouraged to clearly mark their address at the property entrance to assist emergency responders.

Fire District address: 11975 S State Route 69, Spring Valley, AZ 86333
Website: yavcofire.org — Mayer Fire District

Sheriff's Office

Yavapai County Sheriff's Office (YCSO) provides law enforcement for unincorporated Yavapai County, including Goswick Ranch. For non-emergency matters, call the main YCSO line at (928) 771-3260, or contact the Mayer Substation directly at (928) 771-3509. For emergencies, always call 911.

YCSO website: ycsoaz.gov
Email: web.sheriff@yavapaiaz.gov

Wildfire Preparedness

The Bradshaw Mountains and surrounding area are at elevated wildfire risk, particularly from May through monsoon season. The 2017 Goodwin Fire burned 28,516 acres across the Bradshaws over 16 days, triggering evacuations of Mayer and Breezy Pines. Maintain defensible space around your home. Sign up for Yavapai County emergency alerts at ycsoaz.gov — Emergency Alert System.

Flash Flood Preparedness

Big Bug Creek can rise rapidly during monsoon storms (typically July–September). Do not attempt to cross flooded roadways. "Turn Around, Don't Drown."

Post-fire flooding note: Following wildfires, soil in burned areas becomes hydrophobic and runoff increases dramatically. Be especially alert during the first monsoon season after any nearby fire.
