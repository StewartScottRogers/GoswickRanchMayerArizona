Thirty years of navigating the Beltway, the Metro, the GSA approval process, or the Pentagon parking garage. You've earned somewhere that doesn't come with any of that.

Goswick Ranch is a private gated community in Arizona's Bradshaw Mountains. No management company. No approval committee for the color of your front door. No traffic. A volunteer board of your neighbors, a gated private road, 9 acres minimum per lot, and $216 a year in HOA dues.

Property taxes average about $476 a year. Arizona's income tax is 2.5% flat. Military retirement pay is not taxed in Arizona.

You can live here full-time, part-time, or hold it as an investment. No occupancy requirement.

Military Retirement

Arizona does not tax military retirement pay. If you're drawing a military pension, this is a direct, calculable annual savings. The Bob Stump VA Medical Center in Prescott is 30 minutes north on AZ-69, and American Legion Post 78 in Dewey-Humboldt is just 11 miles from Goswick Ranch.

Maryland Estate Tax Note

Maryland is one of the few states that imposes both an estate tax and an inheritance tax. Arizona has neither. For estate planning purposes, Arizona is dramatically simpler for Mid-Atlantic buyers.

Numbers are estimates for general comparison. Consult a licensed real estate agent, tax advisor, and attorney before making any purchase decision.

The Land

What the Land Offers

Four seasons at 4,400–6,000 feet — spring wildflowers, summer in the 80s°F, fall oak color, winter clear and cold with the Milky Way overhead. No humidity. No Beltway. The nearest neighbor is not visible from your property line.

Horses and livestock permitted. Hundreds of miles of Prescott National Forest trail access. Wildlife is a daily companion: deer, elk, bears, eagles. The dark skies are Bortle Class 3–4 — something most DC residents haven't seen outside of a camping trip.

Plan a Visit

How to Get Here from the Mid-Atlantic

By Air

Reagan National (DCA), Dulles (IAD), and BWI all have multiple daily nonstop flights to Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX) — approximately 4–4.5 hours. Philadelphia International (PHL) has multiple daily nonstop PHX flights (~4.5 hours). From PHX: 74 miles north, approximately 70 minutes via I-17 N and SR 69 E.

By Car

DC to Mayer is approximately 2,200 miles via I-81 South / I-40 West / I-17 South — typically a 3-day drive. Many buyers fly for the initial visit and plan the road trip for the actual move.
