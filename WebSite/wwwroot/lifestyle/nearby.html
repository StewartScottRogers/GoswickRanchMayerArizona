Day Trips & Nearby Destinations

Close enough to enjoy regularly. Far enough to feel like genuine country living.

One of Goswick Ranch's most underappreciated assets is its position: close enough to Arizona's best small cities and outdoor destinations to enjoy them regularly, far enough away to feel like genuine country living every evening you come home.

Mayer — The Nearest Town (~2 miles)

Mayer is the closest town to the gate — about one mile from where Poland Road meets AZ-69. It is not a destination so much as a convenience: gas, groceries, hardware, a post office, a pharmacy, and the public library, all within a few minutes of the gate.

  • Gilligan's Mayer Market — 9985 S SR 69 · (928) 632-7311 · Locally owned; all grades; full grocery/C-store; primary local everyday stop
  • Handy Man's Hardware — 10650 S SR 69 · (928) 632-4683 · Local hardware store
  • Circle K — 10068 S SR 69 · (928) 632-5348
  • Smokin' Harley's Steakhouse — 10456 S SR 69 · (928) 632-4255 · Steakhouse in Mayer townsite
  • The Cellar at Creekside — 11255 S SR 69 · (928) 632-4234 · American/Southwestern; live entertainment
  • Legends Harley-Davidson — 10434 S SR 69 · Premier HD dealership; draws riders from across the region
  • Mayer Public Library — 10004 Wicks Ave · (928) 632-7370 · Tue–Sat 9am–5pm
  • Mayer Daze — Annual festival each October (63rd annual in 2024/2025); parade, classic cars, BBQ, vendors, live entertainment; a genuine small-town celebration

For a full grocery run, Prescott Valley (13 miles, 20 minutes) has Safeway, Fry's, Sprouts, Walmart, Target, Costco, Lowe's, and Home Depot. See Local Services for complete Mayer and corridor business listings.

Arcosanti — 11–15 miles (~20 min)

Arcosanti is one of the most distinctive sites in Arizona — and practically in the backyard. Paolo Soleri's iconic experimental city and architectural project near Cordes Junction has been under continuous construction since 1970 as a living laboratory for "arcology" (architecture + ecology). Striking brutalist-influenced concrete vaults and amphitheaters in the high desert.

  • Guided tours Thursday–Monday at 9:30 AM, 11:00 AM, and 2:00 PM
  • Gift shop: Handmade Cosanti wind bells — cast bronze and ceramic on-site; famous worldwide
  • Annual Darkosanti music festival
  • arcosanti.org

Prescott Valley — 13 Miles (~20 min)

The primary hub for medical services, major retail, and shopping on the SR-69 corridor:

  • YRMC East — 7700 E Florentine Rd · (928) 445-2700 · 24/7 ER; Level 4 Trauma Center — the primary hospital for most Goswick Ranch emergencies
  • Walmart, Target, Costco — All on or near SR-69
  • NextCare Urgent Care — 3051 N Windsong Dr; walk-in, no appointment
  • Findlay Toyota Center — Arena concerts and events

Prescott — 27 miles (~35 min)

Arizona's "mile-high city" at 5,319 feet. A walkable historic downtown, Courthouse Plaza, Whiskey Row (a legendary strip of historic saloons including The Palace Restaurant and Saloon, est. 1877 — Arizona's oldest continuously operating bar), a thriving arts scene, excellent restaurants, and one of Arizona's most active outdoor recreation communities.

  • World's Oldest Rodeo — Prescott Frontier Days — Running continuously since 1888; late June through early July (~35,000 visitors annually); professional bull riding, bronc, steer wrestling, team roping, barrel racing
  • Sharlot Hall Museum — 4 acres, 11 exhibit buildings; Governor's Mansion (1864), Fort Misery (oldest log cabin in AZ), Frémont House (1875). Admission $9 adults.
  • Phippen Museum of Western Art — Western art, Arizona Rancher and Cowboy Hall of Fame. Admission $12 adults.
  • Free Summer Concert Series — Courthouse Plaza; most Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday evenings June–September
  • Prescott Farmers Market — Saturdays at 900 Iron Springs Rd
  • Craft breweries & dining — LazyG Brewhouse (24 taps, live music), Superstition Meadery, City Tavern (65-tap self-pour wall), Murphy's Restaurant (1890s general store building)
  • Trader Joe's, Costco, Lowe's, Home Depot — All on or near SR-69 corridor into Prescott
  • Full medical — YRMC West hospital; full specialist practices

Prescott Airport (PRC) — 27–28 Miles

Ernest A. Love Field — often more convenient than Sky Harbor for Goswick Ranch residents. United Express (SkyWest) operates daily service to Los Angeles (LAX) and Denver (DEN) — connections through these hubs open the entire United network. Free long-term parking (no reservations); car rentals at baggage claim (Avis, Budget, Enterprise, Hertz). ~93 flights per month.

Jerome — 40 miles (~50 min)

A National Historic Landmark perched on Cleopatra Hill above the Verde Valley. Once a copper-mining boom town of 15,000+; now a community of galleries, restaurants, wine tasting rooms, and artists. The views of the Verde Valley are exceptional.

  • Jerome Grand Hotel — Former United Verde Hospital (1927); one of Arizona's most famously haunted locations; Asylum Restaurant open daily except Tuesday
  • Caduceus Cellars — Winery founded by Maynard James Keenan (Tool); drove the Verde Valley AVA designation; tasting room in Jerome
  • Galleries, restaurants, historic mine tours, ghost tours

Verde Valley Wine Trail — 35–50 miles

The Verde Valley was designated an American Viticultural Area (AVA) in 2021 and has become one of Arizona's most celebrated wine regions. Approximately 15 tasting rooms along the trail in Cottonwood, Clarkdale, Jerome, and the Page Springs / Oak Creek area.

  • Page Springs Cellars (Cornville) — One of Arizona's most acclaimed wineries; Rhône-style varietals; bistro and tours along Oak Creek
  • Caduceus Cellars / Merkin Vineyards — 14,000 cases/year; Italian and Spanish varietals
  • Alcantara Vineyards (Verde River/Oak Creek confluence) — Live music, events, hay rides, horseback, kayaking, tubing
  • Javelina Leap Vineyard (Page Springs Road, Cornville) — Boutique; known for red wines

Tuzigoot & Montezuma Castle — 27–30 miles

Two of Arizona's most significant and well-preserved prehistoric sites — a natural day-trip pair:

  • Tuzigoot National Monument (Clarkdale) — Sinagua hilltop pueblo on a ridge overlooking the Verde River; 110 rooms, occupied AD 1000–1425; sweeping views of the Verde Valley. Admission $10/adult.
  • Montezuma Castle National Monument (Camp Verde) — Five-story cliff dwelling in a limestone alcove; best-preserved cliff dwelling in North America. Same $10 ticket covers both monuments for 7 days.
  • Dead Horse Ranch State Park (Cottonwood, ~47 miles) — Verde River access; 20+ miles of trails; fishing in stocked lagoons; 180+ bird species; camping; kayaking. $7/vehicle.

Mortimer Farms — 10–15 miles (Dewey)

A working farm with seasonal agritourism: u-pick vegetables and fruit, corn maze, pumpkin patch, farm store. A local institution in Dewey-Humboldt — excellent for families and fall visits.

Sedona — 60 miles (~62 min)

Arizona's most famous destination: red rock formations, upscale restaurants, spas, galleries, and some of the state's best hiking (Cathedral Rock, Bell Rock, Chapel of the Holy Cross). Best on weekdays — popular trailheads reach parking capacity by 7:00 AM on busy weekends. Oak Creek Canyon between Sedona and Flagstaff is spectacular in late October.

Route from Mayer: SR-69 → I-17 north → Exit 298 → SR-179 north into Sedona. Red Rock Pass ($5/day) required for Forest Service trailheads; America the Beautiful passes accepted.

Phoenix Metro — 74 miles (~70 min)

Full metropolitan services, Sky Harbor International Airport (PHX), major medical centers, professional sports, and culture. Route: SR-69 south → Cordes Junction → I-17 south → Phoenix. I-17 between Black Canyon City and Anthem can back up Friday afternoons northbound and Sunday evenings northbound. A new flyover bridge at Cordes Junction / SR-69 is now complete.
