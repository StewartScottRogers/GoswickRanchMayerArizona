Outdoor Recreation

Poland Road ends at Five Corners. Prescott National Forest begins at the gate. The wilderness starts almost immediately.

Goswick Ranch is surrounded by some of Arizona's best outdoor recreation land — Prescott National Forest (Bradshaw Ranger District) essentially surrounds the community, BLM land extends through the Bradshaw foothills, and the Agua Fria National Monument is 15–20 miles south. Residents who drive Poland Road every day are already in the recreation corridor before they reach AZ-69.

Poland Road — The Recreation Corridor Out Your Door

Poland Road (E Poland Rd / CR 58 / NFSR 261) begins at AZ-69 near Poland Junction and climbs ~5.9 miles through Big Bug Creek canyon to Five Corners (elevation 6,926 ft) — the hub where five forest roads converge on the high plateau of Big Bug Mesa. Residents drive this road every day. It is also one of the most interesting and historically rich roads in the Bradshaw Mountains.

Along the way, approximately 5–6 miles up from AZ-69, you pass two of the most significant historic landmarks in the Bradshaws — both visible from the road:

  • Poland Mine mill foundations — remnants of Frank Murphy's 20-stamp mill (built 1902) that processed gold and silver from the Poland Mine
  • The sealed south portal of the Poland-Walker Tunnel — the entrance to an 8,071-foot tunnel built ~1903 to connect the Poland and Walker mining districts; the portal collapsed in 1949 and is now a visible historic landmark

These sites are on private property; observe from the road. See Community History for the full Poland Road mining history.

Big Bug Mesa OHV — 5 Miles / 10–15 Minutes

The Big Bug Mesa OHV Trail System is one of the closest accessible OHV areas to any Arizona community. Located in Prescott National Forest (Bradshaw District) on the northeastern slopes of the Bradshaws:

  • Big Bug Mesa Trail: 5-mile dirt/gravel route, ATV/OHV-rated (max 72-inch width)
  • Big Bug Mesa Semi-Loop: 1.5-mile maintained loop around the mesa rim
  • Dusty, rocky surface; Ponderosa pine forest; panoramic overlook of the Bradshaws
  • Access from Five Corners (top of Poland Road) or via FR 670 from Walker, AZ

At Five Corners, you can also hike the Big Bug Mesa Trail #9434 — a 3×2 mile plateau covered in ponderosa pine, juniper, and ancient alligator juniper. The full mesa exploration runs approximately 12.83 miles with ~890 ft elevation gain.

Prescott National Forest / Bradshaw Ranger District

450+ miles of maintained trail accessible from the Prescott area. Hikers, mountain bikers, equestrian riders, and OHV enthusiasts all find dedicated routes. The Bradshaw Ranger District covers the mountains immediately surrounding Goswick Ranch. Contact: (928) 443-8000.

Selected Named Trails

  • Groom Creek Loop Trail #307 — 9.3 miles RT; climbs to Spruce Mountain (7,684 ft); ponderosa pine and Gambel oak
  • Mount Union Trail #285 — 2 miles RT; summit at 7,979 ft (highest peak in the Bradshaws); access via Walker Road off SR-69
  • Salida Gulch Trail #95 — ~4-mile loop; stream crossings; panoramic views
  • Bradshaw Trail #216 — Multi-mile backcountry through-trail

Dispersed camping throughout most of the Bradshaw District — free, no facilities, 14-day limit, 100 feet from roads, 200 feet from water.

Crown King — 14 Miles via Senator Highway

A historic mining and ranching town at the top of the Bradshaws, accessible via the scenic Senator Highway (Forest Road 52) — best approached as a day trip. The Crown King Saloon has operated since the mining era (originally the Oro Belle Saloon, 1910). The Senator Highway corridor is also one of central Arizona's best fall-color drives — bigtooth maple, Gambel oak, and sumac turn gold and red in mid-to-late October.

Note: Senator Highway requires high-clearance vehicle; 4WD advisable in wet conditions.

Equestrian Access

Goswick Ranch's 9-acre lots and equestrian-friendly CC&Rs make it well-suited for horse owners. Livestock are permitted under RCU-2A zoning. Direct access to Prescott National Forest trails from the surrounding area means riders can leave the property and be in wilderness within minutes.

Foothills Ranch — 7 Minutes from the Gate

7250 S State Route 69, Mayer, AZ 86333 · (928) 379-0260

Guided trail rides into the Bradshaw Mountains along Big Bug Creek — departing from SR-69 just 7 minutes from the Goswick Ranch gate:

  • 1.5-hour trail ride — $95/person · Historic Big Bug Creek; 1890s mining sites
  • 2-hour trail ride — $125/person · Grapevine Springs; Big Bug Mesa views; deer and javelina sightings
  • 3-hour Mountain Adventure — $250/person · Waterfalls; historic and active gold mines

Open 7 days by appointment. Ages 5+. 250 lb weight limit.

Groom Creek Horse Camp

Prescott National Forest equestrian campground (~30 miles) with 30 sites, steel corrals, hitching lines, and trail access. Reservations: recreation.gov. Trails from camp: Wolf Creek Loop (#384, 5 mi), Groom Creek Loop (#307, 9 mi to Spruce Mountain).

  • Los Caballos Veterinary — Large-animal mobile care; 24-hour emergency line: (928) 636-6644
  • Olsen's Grain (Dewey, ~15 miles) — Feed, ranch supply, hay delivery available
  • Prescott Livestock Auction (Chino Valley) — Livestock auction and ranch services

Agua Fria National Monument — 15–20 Miles

BLM manages 71,000 acres of high desert plateau with prehistoric ruins, riparian canyons, and excellent wildlife habitat. Free, open 24/7. Access is via I-17 (same road as Bloody Basin Road, where Mark Goswick Cattle Co. still operates).

  • Badger Springs (I-17 Exit 256) — Permanent water, canyon pools, birding; most popular entry
  • Perry Mesa Archaeological Complex (via Bloody Basin Rd, Exit 259) — Pueblo la Plata: 100+ rooms, occupied AD 1250–1450; petroglyphs; requires high-clearance beyond first 5 miles
  • Dispersed primitive camping — free, no permits for most areas
  • Best months: October–April (summer temps exceed 110°F)

Lynx Lake Recreation Area — 28 Miles

A Prescott National Forest lake and recreation area at 5,500 ft. Fishing (rainbow trout, largemouth bass, crappie, catfish), non-motorized boating (10 HP max), and the 2-mile Lakeshore Loop trail.

Current status: Lynx Lake was intentionally drained in late 2024 for dam valve repairs. The lake was slowly refilling as of March 2026 with the boat ramp still closed. Verify current conditions before planning a visit: Bradshaw Ranger District (928) 443-8000 or fs.usda.gov/prescott

Hunting

Goswick Ranch falls within or adjacent to Game Management Units 20A and 20B. The Bradshaw foothills support:

  • Mule deer — Abundant at all elevations; archery hunting restricted ¼ mile from occupied residences in parts of the unit
  • Merriam's turkey — Higher Bradshaws; Horsethief Basin; spring shotgun + fall archery
  • Javelina — Rocky washes, prickly pear stands
  • Pronghorn — Unit 20A known for trophy pronghorn
  • Gambel's quail — Chaparral foothills; season Oct 17 – Feb 8
  • Black bear and mountain lion — Present throughout the forest

AZ Game & Fish: azgfd.com

Dark Sky Observing

With Bortle Class 3–4 skies, the community's ridgelines and open areas are outstanding sites for amateur astronomy and astrophotography. See Seasons & Dark Skies for details on viewing conditions and best months. The Prescott Astronomy Club holds monthly star parties approximately 28 miles away.

Seasonal Highlights

  • Spring (April–May): Wildflower season — penstemon, lupine, globe mallow in bloom; wildflowers appeared as early as January in the 2025–2026 season
  • Summer (June–Sept): Morning hikes before afternoon monsoon storms; dramatic cloud formations; thunderstorm watching from high vantage points
  • Fall (October–November): Senator Highway fall color drive; hunting season; crown King jalapeno burgers at the saloon; peak conditions mid-to-late October
  • Winter (December–March): Snow at upper elevations; forest roads may require 4WD; best stargazing conditions; Big Bug Mesa Road subject to temporary closure
