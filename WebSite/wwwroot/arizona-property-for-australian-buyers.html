What Goswick Ranch offers — and what it costs to hold

US land investment is not new for Australian buyers. Florida, Hawaii, California — the pattern is well-established. Goswick Ranch in Arizona is one that most Australians haven't looked at yet.

It is a private gated community in the Bradshaw Mountains of central Arizona. Nine-acre minimum lots. $216 USD per year in HOA dues. Property taxes averaging $476 USD per year. Total annual carrying cost: approximately $692 USD (approximately AUD $1,065).

Active listings have ranged from $275,000 to $750,000 USD (approximately AUD $423,000 to $1,155,000 at current exchange rates).

You can own here as an investment, as a holiday base, or for future use. No occupancy requirement.

Visa note: Australian citizens may visit the US for up to 90 days under ESTA, or up to 6 months with a B-2 visitor visa. Owning US property does not confer US immigration rights. If you're considering long-term US residency, that's a separate conversation with an immigration lawyer.

The Investment Case

Carrying costs: At approximately $692 USD per year, the cost to hold is negligible compared to most Australian investment property options. No management company; self-managed HOA.

Appreciation: Yavapai County, AZ has seen strong rural land appreciation. Parcels in the area have shown significant appreciation over recent years. County planning documents identify the SR 69 corridor as a growth area.

Dollar denomination: For Australian investors managing currency exposure, USD land provides diversification out of AUD without the volatility of financial instruments.

Market depth: Phoenix is the 11th largest US metropolitan area. The buyer pool for properties in the Prescott/Mayer corridor is national.

The Wildfire Comparison

Australian buyers have earned the right to ask this question, and we'll answer it like we would for someone who understands fire risk.

The Bradshaw Mountains are ponderosa pine and oak woodland terrain. Wildfire risk exists. The area has had significant fires historically.

The meaningful differences from high-risk Australian bushfire zones:

  • Lot density: 9-acre minimum lots significantly reduce the structural exposure that drives property loss in wildland fire.
  • Insurance market: Arizona's insurance market remains functional. The equivalent of major insurance withdrawal has not occurred.
  • No recent catastrophic event comparable to 2019–2020 season conditions in the immediate vicinity.

Get a current quote from an Arizona-licensed insurer for any specific parcel. Your experience evaluating bushfire risk at home will serve you well here.

Legal and Tax Information for Australian Buyers

  • Visa: ESTA (90 days per visit) or B-2 tourist visa (up to 6 months). Property ownership does not confer immigration rights.
  • FIRPTA: 15% of gross sale price withheld at time of future sale, credited against actual US tax liability.
  • US estate tax: US-sited property owned by non-US persons may be subject to US federal estate tax at death. The US–Australia tax treaty provides some relief.
  • Australian tax reporting: Foreign property and any US-source income must be reported on your Australian tax return.

Consult a cross-border tax advisor (Australian/US qualified) before purchasing.
