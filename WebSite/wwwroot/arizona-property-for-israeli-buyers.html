Three Reasons Israeli Buyers Look at Arizona Land

1. Asset diversification outside Israel. US land is a dollar-denominated, politically stable asset in the world's largest economy. For Israeli investors who already hold diversified portfolios, Arizona ranch land offers geographic and currency diversification — with carrying costs so low (~$692/yr) that the asset can be held long-term without meaningful drag.

2. The investment fundamentals. Yavapai County, Arizona has experienced strong rural land appreciation along the SR 69 corridor. County planning documents identify this as a residential growth area. Recent comparable parcel sales show 57%+ appreciation over a five-year period. The Phoenix metro — 11th largest in the US — drives demand across the surrounding region.

3. A US base for frequent visitors. Israeli citizens can now enter the US without a visa for up to 90 days under ESTA (since September 2023). Israeli-American dual citizens face no stay restriction. Arizona's warm, dry, sunny climate at mountain elevation will feel broadly familiar to buyers from central Israel. Phoenix Sky Harbor is accessible from Tel Aviv Ben Gurion via direct El Al flights or one-stop connections — approximately 14–16 hours total travel time.

For Israeli-American Dual Citizens

If you hold US citizenship or a US Green Card alongside your Israeli passport, the full lifestyle picture is available. There is no visa limitation. You can own, build, and live on the land on any schedule you choose.

Goswick Ranch has no occupancy requirement. You can hold the land as an investment, use it seasonally, or build a permanent home. The community borders Prescott National Forest — hundreds of miles of equestrian and hiking trails accessible from Poland Road.

Important Information for Israeli Buyers

Visa and Stay Rules

Israeli citizens may enter the United States without a visa under the Visa Waiver Programme (ESTA) for up to 90 days per visit — a change that took effect in September 2023. A B-2 tourist visa may permit stays of up to 6 months. Owning US property does not confer any US immigration rights.

Israeli-American dual citizens enter on their US passport and face no stay limitation.

Tax and Legal Considerations for Israeli Buyers

  • FIRPTA: 15% of the gross sale price is withheld at the time of future sale, credited against actual US tax liability.
  • US estate tax: US-sited property owned by non-US persons may be subject to US federal estate tax at death. The US–Israel tax treaty provides some relief.
  • Israeli reporting: Foreign property holdings and any US-source income must be reported to the Israeli Tax Authority. The US–Israel treaty prevents double taxation but does not eliminate reporting obligations.

We strongly recommend consulting a cross-border tax advisor with both Israeli and US expertise before purchasing.

Getting to Goswick Ranch from Israel

By air: Tel Aviv Ben Gurion (TLV) has nonstop service to New York (JFK) and other US hubs; El Al and other carriers serve Los Angeles and other cities with connections to Phoenix. One-stop routing from TLV to Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX) is typically 14–16 hours total travel time. From Phoenix Sky Harbor: 74 miles north — approximately 70 minutes via I-17 N and SR 69 E.
