Rules & Regulations

Community rules and regulations for Goswick Ranch. These rules supplement the CC&Rs and Bylaws. The full Rules & Regulations document will be posted here once received from the board.

Known Standards (from CC&Rs)

The following requirements are established in the recorded CC&Rs. Verify current exact wording in the official documents.

StandardRequirement
Minimum lot size 9 acres (CC&R-imposed — stricter than RCU-2A zoning which requires 2 acres)
Minimum home size 1,500 sq ft living area
Construction type Site-built homes only. No manufactured, modular, or mobile homes.
Zoning classification RCU-2A (Yavapai County Rural Conservation Use)
Gate access Gated community — residents are responsible for managing access for guests and vendors

Architectural Review (ARC)

Proposed improvements to your home or property that are visible from neighboring lots or common areas may require Architectural Review Committee (ARC) approval before work begins. This includes new structures, additions, significant exterior changes, fences, and outbuildings.

To submit an ARC request, use the Architectural Review Request form. The board will confirm the ARC process, timeline, and submission requirements.

Arizona law: Under A.R.S. § 33-1817, if the Architectural Review Committee does not respond to a complete application within 30 days, the application is deemed automatically approved by operation of law. Submit a complete application with all required materials to ensure the clock starts running.

Open Range Law

Yavapai County is open range under Arizona law. This means:

  • Livestock (cattle, horses, sheep, etc.) may legally roam public and private land unless fenced out
  • As a property owner, you are responsible for fencing your property to keep livestock out — not the livestock owner
  • A driver who strikes livestock on a public road may be legally liable if the livestock owner can show the animal was on the road lawfully under open range rules
  • The Goswick family's Mark Goswick Cattle Co. operates in the valley below the subdivision; you may encounter cattle on Goswick Ranch Road or nearby roads

If livestock enter your property, contact the brand inspector or Yavapai County Sheriff for assistance identifying and returning animals to their owner.

Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)

Yavapai County adopted updated ADU rules effective November 19, 2025. Under county rules, the RCU-2A zoning classification now permits:

  • One attached ADU (e.g., in-law suite within or attached to the main home)
  • One detached ADU (separate structure on the property)
  • Maximum ADU size: 1,000 sq ft

Important: The HOA's CC&Rs may impose additional restrictions on ADUs that are stricter than county zoning rules. Review the CC&Rs and consult the board before designing or applying for permits for any ADU project. ARC approval will also be required.

Livestock & Agricultural Use

With 9-acre minimum lots and RCU-2A zoning, Goswick Ranch parcels are eligible for agricultural use. Arizona classifies agricultural property in a separate assessment category (Class 2, assessed at 15% of value rather than the residential 10%), but this benefit applies only if the property's productive agricultural value is lower than its market value — typically only for working ranches or farms with documented agricultural income.

Consult the CC&Rs for any restrictions on livestock types, numbers, or housing on Goswick Ranch lots.
