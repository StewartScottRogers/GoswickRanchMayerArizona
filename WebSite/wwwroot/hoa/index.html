HOA Information

The Goswick Ranch HOA is a self-managed association governed by a volunteer Board of Directors. The Association maintains the gated entrance, enforces the community's CC&Rs and rules, and manages the private roads and common areas within the subdivision.

The annual assessment is $216 — one of the lowest HOA fees you'll find for a gated community with private roads in Arizona.

Arizona law requires us to make governing documents, financial statements, and meeting minutes available to all members. You'll find them all linked below.

2025 Arizona HOA Law Updates

Arizona's legislature passed several updates to the Planned Communities Act (A.R.S. §§ 33-1801 et seq.) in 2025 that affect all HOAs, including Goswick Ranch:

  • SB 1494 — Foreclosure threshold: An HOA may not pursue foreclosure until assessments are delinquent for at least 18 months or $10,000 (whichever comes first).
  • SB 1039 — Meeting recordings: If a board meeting is recorded, the recording must be retained for 6 months and provided to members upon request.
  • SB 1337 — Electronic violations: Members may now respond to violation notices electronically.
  • Assessment cap: Annual assessment increases exceeding 20% require a majority vote of the membership.
  • Annual financial review: HOAs are required to complete an annual financial audit, review, or compilation within 180 days of the fiscal year end.
  • Flag & political sign protections: Extended to protect display of U.S. flags and political signs as specified in state statute.

Architectural Review — Existing Law

Under A.R.S. § 33-1817 (pre-existing, not a 2025 change): if the Architectural Review Committee does not respond to a complete application within 30 days, the application is deemed automatically approved by operation of law. Submit a complete application with all required materials to ensure the clock starts running. See Rules & Regulations for the ARC request process.
