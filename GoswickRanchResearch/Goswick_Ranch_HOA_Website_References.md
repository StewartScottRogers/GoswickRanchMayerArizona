# Goswick Ranch HOA Website — Research & Reference Guide

## 1. HOA Website Best Practices

### Essential Pages Every HOA Site Needs

- **Homepage** — Community introduction, key announcements, welcoming photos, easy navigation
- **About / Community Info** — History, location, what makes the community unique
- **Board of Directors** — Names, roles, contact info for current board members
- **Governing Documents** — CC&Rs, bylaws, rules & regulations (downloadable PDFs)
- **Meeting Minutes & Agendas** — Archive of past meetings plus upcoming agenda
- **News & Announcements** — Community updates, maintenance notices, safety alerts
- **Community Calendar** — Events, meetings, deadlines
- **Contact / Request Form** — How residents reach the board or submit requests
- **Photo Gallery** — Community views, amenities, common areas
- **Resident Resources** — Local service links, emergency contacts, utility info

### Key Design Principles

- Mobile-responsive (62%+ of traffic is mobile)
- Clean, scannable layout with short sections
- Easy document downloads (no login required for public docs)
- Clear navigation menu — 5-7 top-level items max
- Password-protected resident portal for sensitive documents (optional for small HOAs)

## 2. Arizona HOA Legal Requirements

### Document Disclosure (A.R.S. § 33-1805)

- HOAs must make association records **reasonably available** to all homeowners
- Associations have **10 business days** to fulfill a member's request to view records
- **No fees** can be charged for production of records
- Disclosure packet for property sales capped at **$400** (A.R.S. § 33-1806(C))

### Documents That Should Be on the Website

- CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions)
- Bylaws
- Rules and regulations
- Financial statements / annual budget
- Meeting minutes (excluding executive sessions)
- Board member contact information

### Documents That May Be Withheld

- Pending litigation materials
- Personal information of individual homeowners
- Executive session minutes
- Attorney-client privileged communications

### Key Legal References

- A.R.S. § 33-1805 — Association financial and other records
- A.R.S. § 33-1806 — Disclosure requirements and fee caps
- A.R.S. § 33-1258 — Additional disclosure requirements
- azleg.gov/ars/33/01805.htm — Full statute text

## 4. Goswick Ranch Community Profile

### For the "About" Page

- **Type:** Exclusive gated community / subdivision
- **Location:** Mayer, Arizona (Yavapai County), ZIP 86333
- **Setting:** Foothills of the Bradshaw Mountains, overlooking Big Bug Creek
- **Elevation:** ~4,400–6,000 feet
- **Positioning:** Centrally located between Prescott and Phoenix metro
- **Lot Minimum:** 9 acres
- **Home Minimum:** 1,500 sq ft site-built
- **Zoning:** RCU-2A (rural residential)
- **Character:** Rural ranch living, privacy-focused, gated entry
- **Current Online Presence:** None — this will be the first official website

### Mayer, AZ Context

- Unincorporated census-designated place
- Population: ~1,558 (2020 Census)
- Area: ~20 square miles
- Founded in the 1880s as a mining camp and ranching outpost
- Named for Joseph Mayer, a German immigrant who ran a trading post and stage station
- Historic connection to gold and silver mining (Tip Top Mine era)

## 6. Assets Checklist — What to Gather from the Board

### Must-Have Before Building

- [ ] HOA logo (or decision to create one)
- [ ] CC&Rs document (PDF)
- [ ] Bylaws document (PDF)
- [ ] Community rules & regulations
- [ ] Board member names, titles, and contact preferences
- [ ] Community photos (gate, views, common areas, signage)
- [ ] Meeting minutes (at least recent 6–12 months)
- [ ] Annual budget or financial summary (if publicly shared)
- [ ] Approved community color palette or branding (if any)

### Nice-to-Have

- [ ] Community map or plat map
- [ ] Architectural review guidelines and submission form
- [ ] Welcome packet for new residents
- [ ] Emergency preparedness info specific to the community
- [ ] History of the community / developer info
- [ ] Drone or aerial photography of the property
