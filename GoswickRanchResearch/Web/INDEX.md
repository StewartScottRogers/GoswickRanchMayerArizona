## Summary of Key Findings

### 1. Goswick Ranch HOA (File 01)
**What we found:**
- Community confirmed as "Goswick Ranches" subdivision — gated, RCU-2A zoned, 9-acre minimums, site-built only
- Real estate listings price range: $250,000–$599,000 (~5 active listings)
- Marketing tagline in use: "Arizona Urban Ranch Homes"
- No HOA website, no management company, no professional directory presence

**Gaps:**

- Board member names — must come from board
- HOA assessment amount — must come from board
- Subdivision plat recording date — needs Yavapai County Recorder search

### 2. Mayer, Arizona (File 02)
**What we found:**
- Population: ~1,558 (2020); median age 54 years; predominantly White/English-heritage community
- Founded 1882 by Joseph Mayer; railroad arrived 1898; rich mining and ranching history
- Three National Register of Historic Places sites; annual "Mayer Daze" festival
- Elevation ~4,695 ft; semi-arid climate; 4 seasons; ~10 in/year precipitation; monsoon season July–September
- 75 miles north of Phoenix, 27 miles south of Prescott — accessible but genuinely rural

**Website copy potential:** Strong origin story + rural character + accessibility = good "About the Area" content

### 4. Arizona HOA Law (File 04)
**What we found:**
- Governing statute: A.R.S. §§ 33-1801 et seq. (Arizona Planned Communities Act)
- No Arizona statutory requirement for HOAs to maintain a public website
- Records must be "reasonably available" within 10 business days of request
- Resale disclosure requirements (§33-1806): up to $400 fee, 10-day delivery window
- 2025 legislative updates: meeting recordings retained 6 months; foreclosure threshold 18 months/$10,000; flags = political signs
- **Critical finding:** The "100-unit website mandate" cited in some HOA industry articles is California law (AB 1458), NOT Arizona law

**Implication:** Website is best practice, not legally required in AZ. But publishing CC&Rs, bylaws, minutes, and financial info online satisfies disclosure obligations efficiently.

### 5. Reference HOA Websites (File 05)
**What we found (all 3 sites retrieved successfully):**

**Recommended navigation for Goswick Ranch:** Home | About | HOA Information | News & Events | Resident Resources | Contact

### 6. HOA Best Practices (File 06)
**What we found:**
- 11 core features from hoamanagement.com; all applicable to Goswick Ranch with some scaling
- Most important for a small self-managed rural HOA: document downloads, contact info, announcements, mobile-responsive design
- Static site (GitHub Pages/Netlify) is the right choice — no need for full HOA management platform
- **Design:** Earth tones, large hero landscape photo, 16px+ body text, high contrast, max 7 nav items

### 7. Local Geography (File 07)
**What we found:**
- Bradshaw Mountains: 40 × 25 miles; highest peak Mt. Union 7,979 ft; Prescott National Forest; wildlife includes bear, mountain lion, mule deer, javelina
- Big Bug Creek: historic gold placer mining in 1860s–1880s; perennial/seasonal stream; drains from Breezy Pines through Mayer
- 2017 Goodwin Fire (28,516 acres) + subsequent Big Bug Creek flood (July 19, 2017): 109 homes damaged, hydrophobic soil from burn scar caused catastrophic runoff
- Agua Fria National Monument: ~20–30 miles south; free; hiking, petroglyphs, birding, dispersed camping
- Prescott National Forest: ~450 miles of trails; Lynx Lake; Groom Creek Horse Camp — all accessible within 30–60 minutes

**Website content:** Rich material for "About the Area," "Location & Setting," and an emergency preparedness section

### 8. Utilities & Services (File 08)
**What we found:**
- Water: Mayer Domestic Water Improvement District; (928) 632-4113; serves town of Mayer + Poland Junction
- Electricity: APS (Arizona Public Service); (855) 688-2437; outage line (800) 253-9405
- Schools: Mayer Unified School District #43; Pre-K through 12; ~526 students; 12:1 ratio
- Library, recreation center, community services center all identified with contact info

**Gaps:** Gas/propane provider, internet providers, trash hauler, and whether Goswick Ranch uses well water or MDWID — all need board confirmation

### 9. Real Estate Context (File 09)
**What we found:**
- Active listings: 3–5 properties, $250K–$599K, avg ~$290K
- RCU-2A zoning: county minimum is 2 acres; Goswick Ranch's 9-acre minimum is CC&R-imposed (stronger than zoning)
- Median property tax: ~$476/year — dramatically lower than national median ($2,400)
- Zoning ordinance PDF (July 2025 update) publicly available for download
