## 1. Yavapai County Population Growth

| Year | Population | Growth |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 2000 | 168,743 | — |
| 2010 | 211,033 | +25.1% |
| 2020 | 236,209 | +11.9% |
| 2023 | ~249,081–255,911 | +5.4% from 2020 |
| 2024 est. | ~252,013–253,595 | ~1% YOY |

- **20-year average annual growth rate (2000–2023): 2.07%** — well above national average
- Yavapai County is **one of Arizona's fastest-growing counties** — confirmed by Axios Phoenix (January 2025) alongside Pinal County
- **4th most populated county in Arizona**
- **Growth driver: 100% in-migration** — deaths exceed births by ~1,700/year; every net resident arrives from outside
- Domestic net migration (2023–2024): ~3,500 from other U.S. counties; ~645 international
- **31.7% of state-to-state in-migrants are working-age adults (25–54)** — not purely retirees

**City-level growth:**
- Prescott: 45,000+ population; grew 10.6% from 2010–2020
- Prescott Valley: 51,440 (2026 estimate); growing at 2.56%/year; surpassed 50,000 in 2023; growing faster than **91% of similarly sized U.S. cities** since 2000

Sources: Axios Phoenix (Jan 2025) | Neilsberg | USAFacts | Arizona-Demographics.com

## 2. Post-COVID Rural Migration to Arizona

### National Trend
- Americans reporting moving to a different state: +12.5% between 2019–2022
- Americans working from home: increased **four-fold** during the pandemic
- By end of 2020, 48% of Americans would choose to live in a town (17%) or rural area (31%) — up from 39% in 2018 (Gallup)
- A typical remote rural county saw **12 more net migrants per month per 10,000 residents** in year one of the pandemic; sustained (not transient) trend

### Arizona's Position
- 2022: Arizona ranked **4th nationally in net domestic migration** — 77,995 net migrants (behind FL, TX, NC)
- 2023: Arizona ranked **5th** — 62,533 net migrants; 256,203 arrivals from other states; **8th highest in-migration state nationally**

**Top origin states sending residents to Arizona (2022):**
1. California — 74,157 (9.07% of in-migrants)
2. Colorado — 12,232
3. Oregon — 12,093
4. Texas — 17,404
5. Illinois — 10,623

Sources: AZ Big Media | Arizona's Economy | ASU W.P. Carey CCPR

## 6. Arizona Tax Advantages

### State Income Tax
- Arizona flat rate: **2.5%** (as of tax year 2023 — one of the lowest flat-rate taxes in the nation)
- Automatic rate reduction mechanism (SB1318): rate will drop further each year by 50% of Arizona's structural budget surplus

### Full Tax Profile for Retirees

| Tax Type | Arizona Treatment |
|---------|------------------|
| State income tax | Flat **2.5%** |
| Social Security income | **Not taxed** by Arizona |
| Military retirement pay | **Fully exempt** |
| Civil service / state pensions | Up to $2,500 deduction |
| IRA / 401(k) distributions | Taxed at 2.5% flat |
| Property tax | **0.43–0.51% effective rate** (national avg: 0.89%) |
| Estate / inheritance tax | **None** |
| Sales tax | 5.6% state; local up to 10.4%; groceries and prescriptions exempt |

**Senior Property Tax Freeze:** Homeowners age 65+ with qualifying income (~$46,400 single / ~$58,000 joint) can freeze assessed value for 3 years (renewable) — significant protection in an appreciating market.

**For a Goswick Ranch property at $300,000:**
At 0.44% rate: approximately **$1,320/year** in property taxes = $110/month.

Sources: NerdWallet | AARP | SmartAsset | Tax Foundation
