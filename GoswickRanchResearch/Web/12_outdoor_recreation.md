## 1. Prescott National Forest / Bradshaw Ranger District

The Bradshaw Ranger District of Prescott National Forest **essentially surrounds Goswick Ranch**. The forest covers nearly 1.3 million acres total. The Bradshaw District encompasses the Bradshaw Mountain range south of Prescott.

### OHV / ATV Trails

**Big Bug Mesa OHV Trail System** — **~5 miles / 10–15 min from Goswick Ranch**
- Located directly off Highway 69 via Forest Road 670
- Big Bug Mesa Trail: 5-mile dirt/gravel, multi-directional, ATV/OHV-rated (max 72" width)
- Big Bug Mesa Semi-Loop: 1.5-mile maintained loop around the mesa
- Dusty, rocky surface; great overlook of Bradshaws
- Trailforks: https://www.trailforks.com/trails/big-bug-mesa/

**Alto Pit OHV Area** — ~25 miles from Mayer
- 400 acres of ponderosa pine at 6,200 ft; 7 miles NW of Prescott
- ~20 miles of motorized trails; 2 cross-country areas; children's area
- 11 campsites; reservations via Recreation.gov: https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/251846
- Forest OHV total: ~400 miles of motorized trails

## 2. Lynx Lake Recreation Area

**Distance from Mayer: ~28 miles** | Elevation: 5,500 ft | 15 min SE of Prescott

**Fishing**
- Species: Rainbow trout, largemouth bass, crappie, catfish
- 125,000+ visitors/year; regularly stocked with rainbow trout by AZ Game & Fish
- Bag limits: 6 trout, 6 bass/day; unlimited crappie/catfish
- AZ fishing license required (age 14+); annual resident: $37
- Swimming prohibited

**Boating**
- 10 HP max (effectively non-motorized); kayak/canoe/sail popular
- Boat rentals at Lynx Lake Store (late spring–early fall; closed Mondays)

**Hiking**
- Lakeshore Loop Trail: 2-mile circuit; mostly accessible; some steep sections

**Camping**
- Lynx Campground: 39 sites, April–Oct; https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/234762
- Hilltop: 38 sites, ¼ mi south; same rules

Sources: https://www.prescott.com/lynx-lake | https://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/prescott/recarea/?recid=75178

## 3. Horsethief Basin Recreation Area

**Distance from Mayer: ~20–25 miles** | Elevation: ~5,800 ft

**Key fact:** USFS explicitly states "the best route from Prescott is by way of Mayer." This area is accessed *through* Mayer — it is essentially at the community's back door.

**Access:** SR 69 → Antelope Creek Rd → Cordes → FR 259 → Crown King → FR 52 SE ~6 mi

**Trails:** 9 trails totaling ~30 miles; destinations include Horsethief Canyon, Twin Peaks, Castle Creek, Jim Creek

**Fishing:** Horsethief Basin Lake — bluegill, bass, catfish; fish consumption advisory for largemouth bass from this lake.

**Camping:** Hazlett Hollow (15 CCC-era sites); Horsethief Cabin (reservable): https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/234077

Sources: https://www.fs.usda.gov/r03/prescott/recreation/horsethief-basin

## 4. Crown King Ghost Town

**Distance from Mayer: ~14 miles** (Crown King high school students commute to Mayer — closest town)
**Elevation:** 5,771 ft

**History:** Gold mining began 1875; at peak, 500 buildings and $2 million in gold extracted from Crown King Mine alone. Bradshaw Mountain Railroad served 1904–1926.

**Current attractions:**
- **Crown King Saloon**: Originally the Oro Belle Saloon (1910); hotel, café, bar; famous for jalapeño hamburgers; center of community life
- General Store / Post Office
- Historic cemetery
- One-room red schoolhouse (built 1917, still operational)
- Abandoned mines and equipment

**Getting there from Mayer:** SR 69 in Mayer → Pine Flat Road west → Senator Highway south ~26 miles to Crown King
- Senator Highway: maintained gravel; heavily washboarded in places; muddy after rain or snow; high-clearance vehicle strongly recommended; 4WD advisable in wet conditions. Spectacular scenery. ~1–1.5 hours.
- **Bumble Bee / I-17 route** (I-17 Exit 248): More reliable in winter; passes Bumble Bee and Cleator ghost towns along the old railroad bed.

Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_King,_Arizona | https://www.desertusa.com/desert-arizona/arizona-senator-highway.html

## 5. Agua Fria National Monument

**Distance from Mayer: ~15–20 miles south** | Free; open 24/7/365 | No water on site

**Access points from Mayer:** Head south on I-17; monument boundary is essentially at Mayer's southern doorstep.

| Entry | I-17 Exit | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Badger Springs | 256 | ~1 mi gravel to trailhead; most popular |
| Bloody Basin Rd | 259 | Graded first 5 mi; high-clearance/4WD beyond; accesses Perry Mesa |
| 3M Ranch Rd | 262 | Via Cordes Lakes; accesses Sycamore Mesa |

**Trails:** No marked trails — cross-country on rough terrain
- Badger Springs Canyon to Agua Fria River: 0.8 mi; permanent river with pools and cascades; excellent birding
- Perry Mesa Archaeological Complex: Pueblo la Plata (100+ rooms, AD 1250–1450); extensive petroglyphs

**Wildlife:** Saguaro-covered cliffs; permanent river; coyote, bobcat, deer, javelina; Audubon-designated Important Bird Area

**Best times:** October–April; **Avoid** June–early September (110°F+)

BLM contact: Hassayampa Field Office: (602) 867-5400

Sources: https://www.blm.gov/visit/agua-fria | https://www.arizonaruins.com/afnm/main_afnm.html

## 6. Hunting

### Game Management Units

Goswick Ranch falls within or adjacent to **Game Management Units 20A and 20B**.

**Unit 20A:** Bradshaw Mountains, Crown King area, Prescott National Forest; bounded by Iron Springs–Skull Valley–Kirkland roads, south through Crown King and Cordes to I-17.

**Species by unit:**

| Species | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Mule Deer | Common | All elevations; archery hunting restricted ¼ mi from occupied residences in parts of unit |
| Merriam's Turkey | Stable | Higher Bradshaws (ponderosa/mixed conifer); Horsethief Basin; spring shotgun + fall archery |
| Javelina | Common | Rocky washes, prickly pear stands, seep-fed canyons |
| Gambel's Quail | Common | Throughout except highest elevations; chaparral foothills |
| Elk | Rare/transient | Managed at very low density; multi-unit limited draw |
| Pronghorn | Present | Unit 20A known for trophy pronghorn |
| Black Bear | Present | Distributed through forest |
| Mountain Lion | Present | Dog hunting primarily |

**Key access points near Goswick Ranch:**
- Big Bug Mesa: Forest Road 670 via Hwy 69 — essentially at the gate
- Turkey Creek: SR 69 through Mayer
- Crooks Canyon: Forest Road 94 junction area

**2025–2026 Small Game Seasons:**
- Mourning/White-winged Dove: Early season Sept 1–15, 2025; Late season Nov 21, 2025–Jan 4, 2026; daily bag 15
- Gambel's Quail: Oct 17, 2025–Feb 8, 2026; daily bag 15
- Mearns' Quail: Dec 5, 2025–Feb 8, 2026; max 8/day

Sources: http://huntnowarizona.com/unit-20a-arizona/ | https://www.azgfd.com/hunting/where-to-hunt/

## 7. Fishing

### Big Bug Creek (Adjacent to Goswick Ranch)
- Runs through/adjacent to the subdivision
- Primary species: Desert sucker (native to Sonoran desert streams)
- No stocking program; seasonal/perennial flow depending on precipitation
- Named for large beetles that inhabit its banks in June

**AZ Fishing License (2025–2026):**
- Annual resident: $37 | Nonresident: $55
- One-day resident: $15 | Nonresident: $20

## 8. Birding

### Watson and Willow Lakes IBA (Important Bird Area)
**Distance from Mayer: ~30–31 miles** | Audubon Society designated 2011

- 156 species recorded; 51 on a single May survey
- Key species: Wood Duck (50+ breeding pairs), wintering Bald Eagle, resident Peregrine Falcon, Great Blue Heron/Cormorant rookeries, Yellow Warbler, Lucy's Warbler, Green Heron
- Waterfowl (fall): Northern Pintail, Northern Shoveler, Ruddy Duck, Ring-necked Duck
- eBird hotspot: https://ebird.org/hotspot/L270303

### Bradshaw Foothills Species (Near Goswick Ranch Elevation)
- Acorn Woodpecker — common in oak woodland
- Steller's Jay — ponderosa/mixed conifer
- Mountain Chickadee
- Pygmy Nuthatch
- Grace's Warbler — signature pine forest bird
- Painted Redstart — canyon areas (striking red-and-black)
- Zone-tailed Hawk — mimics Turkey Vulture in flight
- Wild Turkey (Merriam's) — year-round at higher elevations
- Riparian species along Big Bug Creek: flycatchers, vireos, warblers, herons

**Resources:**
- eBird Yavapai County: https://ebird.org/region/US-AZ-025/hotspots
- Birding Hotspots: https://birdinghotspots.org/us/arizona/yavapai-county

## 9. Stargazing / Dark Skies

**Bortle Class near Goswick Ranch: estimated 3–4 (Rural Sky)**
- No local streetlights, rural 9-acre minimum lots, no urban core nearby
- Milky Way core dramatically visible spring through early fall
- Zodiacal light sometimes visible; faint nebulae and star clusters naked-eye visible

**Benchmark reading:** A CTA site ~48 miles from Prescott Valley shows Bortle Class 3 with SQM 21.37. The Goswick Ranch area (further from Prescott glow) is likely comparable.

**Nearby dark sky sites:**
| Site | Distance | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Beaverhead Flats Trailhead (PNF) | ~24 mi | 80% confidence dark sky rating |
| Prescott National Forest (dispersed) | Adjacent | Self-select very dark spots |
| Cottonwood (IDA-certified) | ~43 mi | International Dark-Sky Association certified |

**Local astronomy clubs:**
- Prescott Astronomy Club: http://prescottastronomyclub.org — regular stargazing events ~28 mi away
- Astronomers of Verde Valley: Cottonwood area, ~43 mi

**Best viewing:** New moon periods; early summer (stable air); avoid humid monsoon nights (dew on lenses). Hand warmers advised for eyepieces.

Sources: https://nightskyspots.com/stargazing/prescott-valley-az/ | https://www.go-astronomy.com

**Address:** 7250 S State Route 69, Mayer, AZ 86333
**Phone:** (928) 379-0260

Guided trail rides into the Bradshaw Mountains along Big Bug Creek:

| Ride | Duration | Price | Highlights |

### Groom Creek Horse Camp — Prescott National Forest
**Distance: ~30 miles** | Elevation: 6,398 ft

- **Equestrian-only campground** — 30 sites in reservable loops (B & C); Loop A first-come
- Each site: picnic table, steel fire ring, 16'×16' steel corral, overhead tether line (2–4 horses)
- Reservations: https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/234759
- **Trails from camp:** Wolf Creek Loop (#384, 5 mi), Groom Creek Loop (#307, 9 mi to Spruce Mountain)

### Cayuse Equestrian Trailhead — Granite Basin Recreation Area
**Distance: ~28 miles** (7 mi NW of Prescott)

- Restrooms, water for horses, hitching posts, ample trailer parking
- Balancing Rock Trail #349: Shady ponderosa; wildflowers; granite boulders; equestrian-friendly
- Cayuse Loop: Low elevation change; through ponderosa and chaparral

**Prescott National Forest equestrian total: 450+ miles of trails open to horses.**

Sources: https://www.foothills-ranch.com | https://www.fs.usda.gov/r03/prescott/recreation/cayuse-equestrian-trailhead

## 11. Water Recreation

### Lakes Within 30 Miles

**Watson Lake (Prescott, ~30–31 mi):** Kayak/canoe rentals; no motorized boats; Audubon IBA; camping April–Oct; fishing; Peavine Trail along shoreline.

**Willow Lake (Prescott, ~31 mi):** ~400 acres; no motorized boats; canoeing, kayaking, birding, fishing, camping April–Oct.

**Yavapai Lakes (Prescott Valley, ~22 mi):** 4.3-acre community fishing lake; regularly stocked; bass, trout, catfish. No camping, no swimming, no motorized boats.

### Lakes 30–60 Miles

**Lake Pleasant (~40–45 mi south):** 10,000+ acres; full marina (Scorpion Bay + Pleasant Harbor); water skiing, jet skiing, houseboating; best bass fishing in the Phoenix area; 165 campsites; 20 miles of hiking trails; open 365 days.
