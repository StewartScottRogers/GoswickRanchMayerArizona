## 1. Windgate Ranch HOA — windgateranch.org
**Location:** North Scottsdale, Arizona

### Site Navigation Structure
```
Home
Amenities
  └── Tennis/Pickleball
Residents
  ├── Amenity/Gate Access
  ├── Gate Access
  ├── HOA 101
  ├── Document Library
  └── Assessments
Design Review
Facility Rental
Calendar
Real Estate Agents
```
Plus: Login / Register / Site Search

### Key Features
- **Community testimonials** highlighting neighborhood culture
- **Contact information & office hours** prominently displayed in header/footer
- **Pool hours posted** (6 AM – 10 PM)
- **Four-box feature layout** showcasing key offerings with hover links
- **HOA 101 section** — educational resource explaining HOA concepts to residents
- **Document Library** — centralized document access
- **Assessments page** — payment info
- **Design Review page** — guidelines for landscape, hardscape, and architectural modifications
- **Facility Rental** — booking system for community spaces
- **Calendar** — event listings
- **Gate Access** — dedicated page for residents
- **Real Estate Agents** section — info for property professionals

### Design Approach
- **Color:** Teal/green accent (#15655A) — natural, community feel
- **Typography:** Trebuchet MS
- **Layout:** Clean, professional; image-based content boxes with hover effects
- **Gated emphasis:** Gated community designation prominently noted

### Applicability for Goswick Ranch
- HOA 101 section is a great idea for a new website — educates first-time HOA members
- "Real Estate Agents" page could be adapted to "Prospective Buyers" for Goswick Ranch
- Document Library + Assessments combo is the core of what Goswick Ranch needs
- Design Review page maps directly to Goswick Ranch's Architectural Review Committee

## 2. Johnson Ranch HOA — johnsonranch.com/association/
**Location:** San Tan Valley / Queen Creek area, Arizona

### Site Navigation Structure
```
Home
About
  ├── Association Info
  ├── Amenities
  └── New Homeowner Information
Calendar
Forms
Homeowner Portal (external link)
Contact
Resources
  ├── Assessments + Payments
  ├── Home and Landscape Modifications
  ├── Project Updates
  ├── Documents
  ├── Important Contacts
  └── Get Our Emails

```

### Key Features
- **Association Info** — Mission/Vision statements, CC&Rs, Design Guidelines, Enforcement Policy
- **Parking pass system** — Integration with Oversii security for overnight parking requests
- **Amenity access card management** — Electronic key card, $25 replacement fee
- **Document repository** — Governing documents and policies centralized
- **Resident Portal** — External portal link for account management
- **Email subscription** — Dedicated signup for community communications
- **Project Updates page** — Keeps residents informed of ongoing community work
- **Election Results** — Transparent governance (board elections posted)
- **Forms library** — All forms in one place

### Design Approach
- **Colors:** Green (#A3BCA7) and muted red (#C25755) — ranch/agricultural palette
- **Typography:** Trebuchet MS
- **Layout:** Responsive with mobile toggle navigation
- **Contact:** Office hours (Mon–Fri 8am–5pm) and address prominently displayed

### Applicability for Goswick Ranch
- "New Homeowner Information" page is essential — Goswick Ranch is a newer community with residents who may be unfamiliar with HOA structure
- "Important Contacts" resource page maps well to a "Community Contacts" or "Resident Resources" section
- Email subscription list is achievable even without a backend — a mailto: link or simple form initially
- "Home and Landscape Modifications" = Goswick Ranch's Architectural Review process
- Election Results page shows transparency best practice — easy to replicate with static HTML

## Design Inspiration Notes
- Ranch/mountain HOA sites use **earth tones**: greens, tans, warm grays, muted reds
- **Hero images of the landscape** (mountains, open sky, prairie) are the #1 design differentiator
- Keep navigation to 5–7 top-level items maximum
- Typography: Trebuchet MS (both AZ sites) or Montserrat (Canyon Gate) — both work well
- All three sites have clean white backgrounds with colored accent elements
- Consider a simple **sticky header** with the community name/logo and nav
