## A.R.S. § 33-1805 — Association Financial and Other Records

**Key Provisions:**
- Members may **examine association financial and other records at no charge**
- Association has **10 business days** to fulfill a record examination request
- Association has **10 business days** to provide copies of records (may charge **not more than $0.15/page**)
- This statute governs **record access upon request** — it does NOT mandate a public-facing website

**Records That May Be Withheld:**
- Attorney-client communications
- Pending litigation materials
- Closed/executive session board meeting minutes
- Personal, health, or financial records of individual members or employees
- Employee performance, compensation, health, or complaint records

**Records That Must Be Disclosed:**
- Open meeting minutes and annual meeting minutes
- CC&Rs, bylaws, rules and regulations
- Financial statements
- Current operating budget

**2025 Update (A.R.S. §33-1804):**
If a board **records** a meeting that is open to members, they must:
- Retain the unedited recording for at least **6 months**
- Make the recording available to any member upon request

## A.R.S. § 33-1806 — Resale Disclosure Requirements

**What associations must provide to purchasers within 10 days of notice of a pending sale:**
- Bylaws, rules, and declaration (CC&Rs) documents
- Dated statement with association contact information
- Current assessment amounts owed by the seller
- Insurance coverage details
- Reserve fund amounts held by the association
- Information about any CC&R violations on the property
- Pending litigation details affecting the unit
- Current operating budget
- Most recent annual financial report (or summary if over 10 pages)
- Most recent reserve study, if available
- Summary of pending lawsuits involving the association
- Statement acknowledging governing documents constitute a contract and foreclosure acknowledgment

**Fee Limits:**
- Up to **$400 aggregate** for resale services
- Optional rush fee: $100 for 72-hour service
- Update fee: $50 if 30+ days since original disclosure
- Fees collected at escrow closing only; charged once per transaction

**Penalties:** Improper fee collection → civil penalties up to $1,200

## A.R.S. § 33-1803 — Assessments and Late Fees
- HOAs may collect assessments but must cap late fees at **$15 or 10% of the unpaid amount**, whichever is greater
- Associations cannot increase regular dues more than **20% annually** without member majority approval

---

## A.R.S. § 33-1804 — Open Meetings
- Boards must provide **at least 48 hours' advance notice** of open meetings, along with an agenda
- (2025 update: recordings of open meetings must be retained 6 months and provided on request)

---

## A.R.S. § 33-1807 — Foreclosure Threshold (2025 Update)
- Judicial lien foreclosures prohibited until delinquency reaches either **18 months** or **$10,000**, whichever occurs first

---

## A.R.S. § 33-1818 — Parking Restrictions
- HOAs with pre-2015 declarations may no longer enforce parking restrictions on **public roads** unless membership votes to maintain this authority by June 30, 2025

---

## A.R.S. § 33-1808 — Political Signs / Flags (2025 Update)
- Associations cannot prohibit indoor or outdoor **political sign or flag** displays on member property
- May regulate size and number within limits matching local ordinances
- **2025 expansion:** Flags are now included in the legal definition of "political sign"

---

## A.R.S. § 33-1816 — Solar Devices
- Associations cannot prohibit solar energy device installation or use
- Reasonable placement restrictions are permitted

---

## A.R.S. § 33-441 — For Sale Signs
- Any covenant prohibiting "for sale" signs is unenforceable

## Governing Document Hierarchy (Arizona)
1. Articles of Incorporation (establishes legal status)
2. Declaration / CC&Rs (recorded core restrictions)
3. Bylaws (operational procedures)
4. Rules and Regulations (day-to-day policies — most flexible)

---

## Mandatory Record Retention (Board Obligations)
HOAs must **permanently maintain:**
- CC&Rs, Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, Deeds, Easements
- Meeting minutes (A.R.S. 10-11601)
- Fine notices (kept during ownership + 1 year after)

**Best practice (digital):** Transition from paper to secure digital storage; establish redaction procedures for privileged info; develop document retention policy with legal counsel

## Dispute Resolution
- Arizona Department of Real Estate administers the **HOA Dispute Process** via administrative hearings (no court needed)
- Key contacts: AZ Dept. of Real Estate, AZ Attorney General, AZ Corporation Commission (nonprofit filings)

## Website/Online Disclosure — Current Arizona Status

**Important finding:** Arizona does NOT currently have a general statutory requirement for HOAs to maintain a public-facing website. The disclosure obligations under A.R.S. §33-1805 require that records be "reasonably available for examination" upon written request — this can be satisfied by allowing in-person review.

**Note on the "100-unit" threshold referenced in HOA industry sources:** Some HOA industry articles reference a January 1, 2025 requirement for associations with **100+ units** to maintain a website with a resident portal. This appears to reference **California** law (AB 1458 / Civil Code §5200), NOT Arizona law. Goswick Ranch is a small community well under 100 units regardless.

**Practical implication for Goswick Ranch:** Building a website is best practice and provides excellent member service, but it is not legally mandated under current Arizona law. However, making the CC&Rs, bylaws, meeting minutes, and financial information available on the website satisfies the spirit of A.R.S. §33-1805 and streamlines compliance.

---

## Documents That Must Be Publicly Available (Best Practice / Statute-Aligned)
Per A.R.S. §33-1805 and §33-1806, the website should include:
1. CC&Rs (Declaration)
2. Bylaws
3. Rules and Regulations
4. Current operating budget
5. Most recent annual financial report/summary
6. Most recent reserve study (if one exists)
7. Board meeting minutes (open sessions only, not executive sessions)
8. Board member contact information
9. Assessment amounts
