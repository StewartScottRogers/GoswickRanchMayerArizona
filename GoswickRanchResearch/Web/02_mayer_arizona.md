## History

### Founding
- Original Yavapai name: **"Wi:kidoʼyoʼ"**
- Founded **1882** by **Joseph Hoffmayer** (went by Joseph Mayer), a gold prospector and entrepreneur from New York
- His wife **Sarah Belle Wilbur** became the town's first postmistress in 1884
- Mayer granted railroad right-of-way access; **Southern Pacific Railroad began service in 1898**
- A brickyard and Mayer Business Block (1902) with saloon, barber shop, and general store were established
- Mayer funded two schools — one in town, one for miners' children
- Joseph Mayer died November 28, 1909 (accidental self-inflicted gunshot); "the largest funeral ever held in this city"

### Key Historical Events
- **1890:** Original community largely destroyed by a dam collapse
- **1917:** Mayer Smokestack constructed (129.5 feet tall)
- **May–June 1942:** Mayer Assembly Center — one of 17 temporary WWII Japanese American detention camps; held 245 people (69 families) before transfer to Poston
- **2017 Goodwin Fire:** Sparked June 24, burned 28,516 acres over 16 days; Mayer and Breezy Pines evacuated June 27; 9,000+ residents evacuated countywide. Post-fire flooding of Big Bug Creek destroyed/damaged 109 homes (see also `07_local_geography.md`)

### National Register of Historic Places (3 sites)
1. Mayer Apartments
2. Mayer Business Block
3. Mayer Red Brick Schoolhouse
(Also mentioned: Mayer Market, Hotel, Smokestack)
