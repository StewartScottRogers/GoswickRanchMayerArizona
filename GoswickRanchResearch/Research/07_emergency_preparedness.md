## Background: Why This Matters for Goswick Ranch

Goswick Ranch sits approximately 1 mile east of AZ Route 69 near Mayer, AZ 86333, in the Bradshaw Mountains foothills of Yavapai County. The community faces compounding risks:

- **Wildfire:** The Bradshaw Ranger District of Prescott National Forest borders the area. The 2017 Goodwin Fire burned 28,516 acres nearby. The April 2022 Crooks Fire burned 9,402 acres near Mt. Union (~10 miles south of Prescott). The Brady Fire (February 2025) burned 400 acres near Pine Flats, just west of Mayer, triggering GO evacuations. APS has identified Mayer as subject to Public Safety Power Shutoffs during extreme fire danger.
- **Post-fire flooding:** The 2017 Goodwin Fire scar caused catastrophic flooding of Big Bug Creek on July 19, 2017, damaging 109 homes in under 4 hours. This is the defining local disaster event.
- **WUI classification:** The community is firmly in the Wildland-Urban Interface, which affects insurance availability, fire response, and property hardening requirements.

## 1. Wildfire Preparedness

### 1.1 Arizona Defensible Space Requirements

Arizona follows a three-zone model. Local ordinances may be stricter; Mayer Fire District can advise.

**Zone 0 — Immediate Zone (0–5 feet from structure):**
Not yet legally required statewide but strongly recommended. Use non-combustible materials (gravel, pavers, concrete) instead of wood mulch; remove dead weeds, dry grass, and debris; eliminate combustibles stored against the house.

**Zone 1 — Intermediate Zone (5–30 feet):**
- Outbuildings and LPG/propane tanks need 10 feet of cleared space to bare soil, plus an additional 10-foot perimeter
- Remove low-hanging limbs (up to 6 feet from ground on trees)
- Vertical spacing: shrub height × 3 = required clearance to lowest tree branch
- Remove dead plants, grass, and debris; keep grasses mowed short

**Zone 2 — Extended Zone (30–100 feet, or to property line):**
- Space trees so crowns have 18+ feet between them
- Limb trees 6–10 feet from ground
- Remove dead vegetation and branches
- Interrupt fire's path by removing ladder fuels

**Resources:**
- AZ Fire Marshals Association — Creating Defensible Spaces: https://www.azfiremarshals.org/creating-defensible-spaces
- University of Arizona Extension AZ1290 (free PDF): https://extension.arizona.edu/sites/extension.arizona.edu/files/pubs/az1290.pdf
- Yavapai County Wildfire Information for Homeowners: https://extension.arizona.edu/publication/yavapai-county-wildfire-information-homeowners

### 1.3 Fire Restriction Levels

Arizona uses a Stage system managed by DFFM on state/private lands, with separate orders from the U.S. Forest Service on federal lands.

**Stage 1:** Campfires allowed only in developed sites with fire rings; no open burning; no smoking while traveling through wildland areas.

**Stage 2:** No campfires anywhere; stricter prohibitions including fireworks, target shooting with tracer/incendiary ammunition, chainsaws without spark arrestors.

**Current status resources:**
- Yavapai County fire ban: https://www.yavapaiready.gov/FireBan
- DFFM fire restrictions: https://dffm.az.gov/fire-restrictions
- Prescott National Forest alerts: https://www.fs.usda.gov/r03/prescott/alerts
- Bradshaw Ranger District: (928) 443-8000

Human-caused fires account for up to 85% of wildland incidents.

### 1.5 Firewise USA Program

**Benefits for Goswick Ranch:**
- USAA homeowners insurance discount (up to 5%) for policyholders in recognized communities
- Regional carriers in Prescott/Yavapai area have offered 5–15% premium credits
- Under Arizona HB2384, insurers must factor local Firewise designations into rates for municipalities under 150,000 population
- Community cohesion and structured annual preparedness activity

**Requirements to become recognized:**
1. Form a board/committee with a Resident Leader (8–2,500 dwelling units required)
2. Conduct a community wildfire risk assessment with a local expert
3. Develop a 3-year action plan
4. Hold at least one annual wildfire education event
5. Invest a minimum of one volunteer hour per dwelling unit per year in risk reduction
6. Report annually through the Firewise Portal

- Apply through: https://portal.firewise.org
- DFFM Firewise page: https://dffm.az.gov/fire/prevention/firewise
- AZ state liaison (Northern AZ/Yavapai County): Aaron Casem, acasem@dffm.az.gov, (602) 771-1403
- Yavapai Firewise (PAWUIC): (928) 277-8032, pawuic1@gmail.com; https://yavapaifirewise.org

### 1.6 Free Slash Drop-Off Program
Yavapai County offers free slash/brush disposal each year (typically March–June) at county Transfer Stations. The **Mayer Transfer Station** (11250 S Antelope Creek Rd) is one of seven locations. 2025 program: March 20–June 15.

## 2. Evacuation Planning

### 2.1 The Ready, Set, Go! Program

All 15 Arizona county sheriffs — including Yavapai — have adopted Ready, Set, Go! (RSG). Three levels:

**READY (ongoing):** Always be prepared. Maintain defensible space. Build/maintain a go kit. Make a family evacuation and communication plan. Know your evacuation zone. Sign up for alerts.

**SET (threat approaching):** Significant danger present. Consider voluntarily evacuating early — especially for those with pets, livestock, mobility limitations, or medical equipment. Gather go kit, load vehicles, confirm communications plan. Early evacuators are safer and free routes for others.

**GO (imminent danger):** Evacuate immediately. Follow designated evacuation routes. Do not wait for official orders if you feel unsafe. Do not return until officials declare it safe. Call 911 if you need evacuation assistance.

- AZ EIN page: https://ein.az.gov/ready-set-go

### 2.2 Yavapai County Evacuation Zones

Zones use a combination of letters (geographic area) and numbers (sub-zones). During an emergency, specific zones are placed on SET or GO rather than broad geographic areas.

**Brady Fire example (Feb 2025):** Only zones YCU-2174 and YCU-2216 (Pine Flats) were placed on GO — neighboring communities were unaffected.

**How to find your zone:**
1. Visit https://protect.genasys.com — type your address
2. Download the free **Genasys Protect** app (App Store or Google Play)
3. Map shows your zone outlined in green; zone status displayed below zone ID
4. More info: https://www.yavapaiready.gov/Resilience/Prepare/Know-Your-Zone

### 2.3 Emergency Alert System — Smart911 / RAVE

Yavapai County uses **Smart911 / RAVE Alert** (replaced Everbridge in late 2022 — previous Everbridge registrants must re-register).

**Sign up:**
- Online: https://www.smart911.com/smart911/ref/reg.action?pa=yavapaiaz
- Text: **alertYAVAPAI** to **78015**
- Create a profile with home, work, and family contacts; choose notification methods (text, call, email, or app)

**Also:**

- YCSO main contact: (928) 771-3260
- Yavapai Firewise Alert page: https://yavapaifirewise.org/alert-yavapai

### 2.4 Evacuation Routes from Goswick Ranch / Mayer Area

The primary evacuation route from Goswick Ranch (~1 mile east of SR 69):
1. Exit the community onto E Poland Rd, then turn onto SR 69
2. **North on SR 69** toward Prescott Valley/Prescott (preferred — away from I-17 interchange wildfire zones)
3. South on SR 69 to I-17, then north toward Prescott or south toward Phoenix

**Key vulnerability:** SR 69 is the **only paved evacuation corridor for the Mayer area**. If SR 69 is compromised, there is no readily available alternative paved route.

**Real-time road conditions:**
- Call **511** from any phone
- Website: https://az511.gov
- AZ511 mobile app (iOS/Android)
- ADOT current restrictions: https://azdot.gov/projects/current-road-restrictions

### 2.5 Large Animal / Livestock Evacuation

**L.A.S.E.R. (Large Animal Shelter & Emergency Readiness):**
Activated during the Brady Fire (Feb 2025) for equines, poultry, goats, pigs, llamas, and alpacas from Pine Flats area.

**Equine Emergency Evacuation (EEE):**
Assists equine owners in wildfire, flood, and other emergencies. Contact Yavapai Firewise (928) 277-8032 for referral.

## 3. Flood Risk and Awareness

### 3.1 The 2017 Goodwin Fire / Big Bug Creek Flood — The Defining Event

This is essential context for every Goswick Ranch resident.

**The Fire:** June 24–July 10, 2017. Goodwin Fire burned 28,516 acres in the Big Bug Creek watershed. The fire created a **hydrophobic soil layer** — burned soil that repels water instead of absorbing it.

**The Flood — July 19, 2017:**
- 4:15–4:45 PM: 1–2 inches of rain in 30 minutes over the burned scar
- 5:51 PM: Big Bug Creek gauge hit **9.8 feet** (7,291 cubic feet/second)
- 5:45–8:00 PM: Catastrophic flooding through Mayer, Spring Valley, Diamond Valley
- Multiple swift-water rescues performed

**The Damage:** 109 homes damaged; 13 with major damage or total loss. Stagecoach mobile home park severely damaged.

**The Lesson:** A modest monsoon storm that would normally be absorbed became catastrophic because the burn scar eliminated soil infiltration. Recovery of soil hydrology takes 1–3 years after a fire.

**Sources:** https://www.weather.gov/fgz/MayerBigBugFlood2017 | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodwin_Fire

### 3.2 Post-Fire Flood Risk — Ongoing Concern

Any significant fire burning in the Big Bug Creek watershed upstream of Mayer creates elevated flood risk during the subsequent monsoon season(s):
- Burn scars increase flood risk for 1–3 years
- Debris flows (not just water but mud, rocks, trees) are most dangerous and travel at high speed
- Monsoon rains are the trigger — 1 inch in 30 minutes over a burn scar is sufficient

**Real-time monitoring:**
- Big Bug Creek gauge at SR 69 (station YMBA3): https://yavapaiaz.gov/ycflood/Weather/Weather-Stations-Gauges/ArtMID/6450/ArticleID/10997/3705-Big-Bug-Creek-at-SR69-YMBA3
- Yavapai County ALERT System (5-minute updates): https://gis.yavapaiaz.gov/alert/
- Flood After Fire page: https://www.yavapaiaz.gov/Resident-Services/Flood-Control/Flood-Safety/Local-Flood-Hazard/Flood-After-Fire

### 3.3 FEMA Flood Maps — Big Bug Creek

- **Zone AE** — Special Flood Hazard Area (1% annual probability) in developed areas
- FIRM map numbers: **04025C2488H** and **04025C2470H** (effective March 2, 2015)
- Look up any property: https://msc.fema.gov/portal/search

Properties within Zone AE may be required by lenders to carry flood insurance if they have a federally backed mortgage.

### 3.4 Flood Insurance

Standard homeowners insurance **does not cover flood damage.** Flood insurance must be purchased separately.
- NFIP policies: check community participation at https://www.floodsmart.gov
- Standard 30-day waiting period may be waived for post-wildfire flooding from adjacent federal land
- Yavapai County Flood Insurance page: https://www.yavapaiaz.gov/Resident-Services/Flood-Control/Flood-Protection-Insurance-and-Safety/Flood-Insurance

## 5. APS Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS)

APS (Arizona Public Service) has identified **Mayer as subject to PSPS** during extreme fire weather conditions (high winds, low humidity, critically dry fuels).

**What to prepare:**
- Keep a minimum of 3 days' supply of essential medications refrigerated or stored cool
- Have backup charging solutions for medical equipment
- Stock water supplies (important if pump is electric-powered)
- Refrigerate/freeze extra water and food in advance of forecast weather events
- APS PSPS page: https://www.aps.com/en/Utility/Outage/Public-Safety-Power-Shutoff
