## Section 1: Setting and Location

**H2:** Where We Are

> Goswick Ranch sits on **E Poland Road** in the foothills of the **Bradshaw Mountains**, Yavapai County, Arizona. The ranch is approximately 1.5 miles from the AZ Route 69 / Poland Road junction — Mayer, the nearest town, is one mile away.
>
> At elevations ranging from approximately 4,400 to 6,000 feet, the community occupies a transitional zone between chaparral and ponderosa pine — one of the most biologically diverse and visually dramatic landscapes in the state.

**Key facts block (sidebar or callout):**
- **County:** Yavapai County, Arizona
- **ZIP Code:** 86333 (Mayer)
- **Elevation:** ~4,400–6,000 ft
- **Nearest town:** Mayer, AZ (1 mile)
- **Prescott:** 27 miles north (~35 min)
- **Phoenix Sky Harbor:** 74 miles south (~70 min)
- **Zoning:** RCU-2A (Rural Community Unincorporated)

**Access directions:**
From AZ Route 69, turn west onto **E Poland Road** (also signed Poland Junction Rd). Travel approximately 1.5 miles; look for the Goswick Ranch sign and gate. The gate is accessed via lockbox.

## Section 2: The Historic Goswick Ranch

**H2:** A Historic Working Ranch

> Multiple real estate listings describe Goswick Ranch subdivision lots as "originally part of the historic Goswick Ranch" — the modern HOA community was carved from an actual working cattle ranch. The property includes references to original buildings, barns, and homes from the ranch era.

### The Goswick Family

The Goswick family has deep, multi-generational roots in Yavapai County:

| Name | Notes |
|---|---|
| William Isaac Goswick (b. 1836, TN) | Earliest Arizona ancestor; mail carrier on the Camp Verde–Payson route, 1884–1914 |
| George Thomas Goswick (b. 1859, MO) | Son of William Isaac; lived in Humboldt, Yavapai County |
| Charles "Giles" Goswick (b. 1888, TX) | Government lion hunter; operated in the Bradshaw Mountains area |
| George Allen Goswick (b. 1919, Camp Verde) | Confirmed in 1930 census in Mayer, Yavapai County — the strongest link to the ranch |

**Mark Goswick Cattle Co** remains an active business at Bloody Basin Rd, Mayer, AZ 86333 — continuing the family's ranching identity in the same corridor.

> **Note for copy:** The specific connection between the Goswick family and the subdivision naming is documented through census records and the active cattle company; a formal title-chain link has not been confirmed. Write copy as "believed to be" or "named for" rather than as a documented fact.

## Section 3: Community Character

**H2:** What Goswick Ranch Is

> Goswick Ranch is a **self-managed, privately gated** community of approximately 25–35 parcels. Minimum lot size is **9 acres** — far above the county's 2-acre baseline. All homes are site-built. The community's internal roads are private and subdivision-maintained.

### The Gate

The entrance gate is a custom ornamental iron double gate featuring **full-height cowboy and cowgirl silhouettes** flanking a sun/wheel design at center — a photographable landmark and distinctive symbol of the community's ranch character. It is accessed via a physical lockbox (not an electronic keypad).

### Road Network

Internal streets include:
- S Goswick Ranch Rd (main road)
- S Hawk Mountain Trail
- S Miners Pick Rd
- E Vaca Bonita Trail / Rd
- Bullhorn Rd
- E Pearl Mountain Rd
- E Elizabeth Mine Rd (mining-heritage name)

## Section 4: The Land

**H2:** Geography and Natural Setting

> The Big Bug Creek watershed drains through the community. At nearly 6,000 feet in the Bradshaw Mountains, residents experience genuine four seasons — spring wildflowers, summer monsoons with dramatic lightning and towering cumulus, fall color on the oaks and cottonwoods, and occasional winter snow.

**Wildlife present in the area:**
Mule deer, elk, black bears, mountain lions, coyotes, javelina, bobcats, golden eagles, red-tailed hawks, great horned owls, Gambel's quail, and dozens of songbird species during spring migration.

**Dark skies:**
The ranch is in a Bortle Class 3–4 zone — the Milky Way is clearly visible on clear nights with no light pollution. This is among the darkest accessible sky in Yavapai County.
