## What We're Trying to Do

The Pacific Northwest pitch is built on two pillars: **grey weather and outdoor identity.**

Seattle averages 150+ rainy days a year and only 71 sunny days. Portland is similar. For retirees who have spent 30–40 years in this climate, the prospect of 300 days of sun is not abstract — it is a quality-of-life calculation they have been running in their heads for years.

The outdoor identity bridge is equally important. PNW residents hike, ride, camp, and value undeveloped land. Goswick Ranch's trail access, wildlife, equestrian use, and dark skies speak directly to this identity. The Bradshaws are not the Cascades, but they are real mountains with real access.

**The right outcome:** A retired tech worker from Bellevue, a retired teacher from Portland, an independent contractor from Eugene who has been talking about "leaving the rain" for a decade reads this and thinks *"This is exactly what I've been picturing."*

### Washington vs. Oregon — Different Pitches

**Washington (Seattle) buyers:** No state income tax — the financial case is primarily property taxes and COL, not income tax. Lead with the weather and the equity reallocation.

**Oregon (Portland) buyers:** 9.9% top marginal rate — the income tax case is significant. Arizona's 2.5% flat is a major improvement. Both the weather and the financial case work.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Honor the outdoor identity.** Hiking, equestrian, wildlife, dark skies — all of these are things this audience actively values. The Bradshaw Mountains are not foreign to a PNW hiker.
- **Acknowledge the equity story.** Seattle homeowners have seen dramatic appreciation. Bay Area-level equity exits are possible for long-term Seattle owners.
- **Speak to environmental values.** Many PNW retirees care about undeveloped land, water quality, wildlife. The ranch's location adjacent to Prescott National Forest resonates.
- **Name the grey.** It's the elephant in the room and every Seattleite knows it. You can say it directly.
- **Note the Milky Way.** For someone who has lived under Seattle's marine cloud cover for 30 years, dark skies hit hard.

### DON'T
- Don't use "escape the rain" framing mockingly. PNW residents are proud of their region.
- Don't assume they don't know Arizona. Many PNW residents vacation in AZ or have driven I-5 south.
- Don't ignore the Oregon income tax case for Portland buyers — it's significant.
- Don't assume everyone is tech-wealthy. Eugene and Tacoma have a different income profile than Bellevue.

### H2: The Outdoor Life Doesn't Change — Just the Weather

You don't have to give up the outdoor life to leave the Pacific Northwest.

Goswick Ranch borders Prescott National Forest. Hundreds of miles of maintained equestrian and hiking trails are accessible from the Poland Road corridor. Deer, elk, bears, and eagles are daily companions. The terrain is varied — high desert, ponderosa pine, creek drainages.

The weather through summer peaks in the 80s°F while Phoenix sits at 110°F+. Monsoon season brings dramatic afternoon thunderstorms from mid-June through September — intense, brief, and followed by cool air that smells nothing like Seattle rain and everything like it.

In winter: 40s–50s°F days. Occasional snow. And the Milky Way overhead on clear nights — Bortle Class 3–4 dark sky, something most Seattle and Portland residents haven't seen from their own property since childhood, if ever.

### H2: How to Get Here from the Pacific Northwest

**By air:**
Seattle (SEA) and Portland (PDX) both have multiple daily nonstop flights to Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX). Flight time: Seattle ~2.5 hours; Portland ~2 hours. From PHX, the community is 74 miles north — approximately 70 minutes via I-17 N and SR 69 E.

**By car:**
Seattle to Mayer is approximately 1,450 miles via I-5 South to US-97 South / I-84 East through Portland, then south through Nevada to I-40 West, then I-17 South to SR 69. A 2–3 day road trip. Portland to Mayer is approximately 1,250 miles. Many buyers make this as a scouting trip.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**How many sunny days does Goswick Ranch get?**
The Mayer, AZ area averages approximately 300 days of sun per year — comparable to the broader central Arizona high country. Compare to Seattle's ~71 sunny days and Portland's ~144.

**Does Oregon have lower taxes than Arizona?**
No. Oregon's top income tax rate is 9.9%; Arizona's is 2.5% flat. For Oregon retirees, the savings can be significant — over $5,000/year on $80,000 in income.

**Can I own the property part-time?**
Yes. No occupancy requirement. Seasonal, part-time, or investment use all permitted.

**What's the summer climate like?**
Daytime highs typically in the 80s°F at elevation, with monsoon season mid-June through September. Dramatically different from Phoenix (110°F+) and milder than most of Arizona's lowlands.
