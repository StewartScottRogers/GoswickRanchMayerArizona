## What We're Trying to Do

1. **Israeli-American dual citizens or US permanent residents:** Full relocation is viable. The retirement/lifestyle pitch applies in full. Many are already splitting time between Israel and the US.

3. **Vacation / second-home buyers:** ESTA (90 days per visit) or B-2 tourist visa (up to 6 months). Arizona's climate is familiar — warm, dry, desert mountain terrain. Shorter flight than Florida from Israel.

**The right outcome:** An Israeli tech entrepreneur who has been diversifying into USD assets, an Israeli-American considering a permanent US base, or a Tel Aviv family looking for a US vacation property reads this and thinks *"This is a serious option I hadn't considered."*

## Critical Legal Considerations

**These must be addressed upfront. Israeli buyers — especially those in finance or tech — will know to ask.**

### Visa and Stay Rules
- Israeli citizens were admitted to the US Visa Waiver Programme (ESTA) in **September 2023**. Israeli passport holders can now enter the US for **up to 90 days** per visit without a visa.
- A B-2 tourist visa may allow stays of **up to 6 months** per visit.
- Owning US property does not confer any US immigration rights.
- Israeli-American dual citizens enter on their US passport — no stay restriction applies.
- For full-time US relocation without a US passport, a US immigration pathway is required (investor visa, spousal visa, etc.).

### US Tax on Israeli Buyers
- Israeli citizens who own US property are subject to standard foreign-buyer US tax rules: FIRPTA withholding on sale (15% of gross sale price, credited against actual liability), and US federal estate tax exposure on US-sited property at death.
- Israel and the United States have a tax treaty in force. The US–Israel tax treaty provides some estate and income tax relief, but cross-border professional advice (Israeli accountant + US tax attorney) is essential.
- Rental income from US property must be reported on both Israeli and US tax returns; the treaty prevents double taxation but does not eliminate reporting obligations.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Be direct and specific.** Israeli buyers — particularly those from tech and finance — respond well to data, facts, and no-nonsense framing. Skip the American promotional style.
- **Lead with the numbers.** $692 USD per year in carrying costs is a concrete, striking figure. Use it early.
- **Acknowledge the security/diversification angle without being sensationalist.** Many Israeli buyers are explicitly motivated to hold assets outside Israel. State this matter-of-factly: US property is a geographically and politically diversified asset.
- **Distinguish between dual-citizen and non-citizen buyers.** Israeli-Americans face different visa, tax, and framing scenarios than Israeli nationals. The page should clearly address both.
- **Note Arizona's climate.** Israeli buyers are already acclimated to a hot, dry, sunny climate. Arizona's mountain-desert terrain at 4,400–6,000 ft elevation (cooler than the Phoenix valley) may be unfamiliar — explain it.
- **Phoenix proximity.** Phoenix's Israeli-American community and El Al / direct flight connectivity matter. Phoenix Sky Harbor has direct or one-stop routing from Tel Aviv Ben Gurion (TLV).

### DON'T
- Don't treat Israeli buyers as a monolithic group — the dual-citizen segment and the Israeli-national investment segment have different constraints.
- Don't ignore FIRPTA, US estate tax, and Israeli tax reporting obligations. Israeli buyers with US assets are used to cross-border complexity.
- Don't use soft or vague language about security concerns. State the factual situation plainly.
- Don't overlook the Israeli-American diaspora in Phoenix/Scottsdale as a referral and social proof opportunity.
