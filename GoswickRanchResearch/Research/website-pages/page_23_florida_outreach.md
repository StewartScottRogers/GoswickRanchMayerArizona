## What We're Trying to Do

Florida is a unique pitch because many of its retirees **came from somewhere else to retire there.** They made the move. They discovered Florida's realities — hurricane season, humidity, escalating insurance costs, increasing density — and are reconsidering.

This is not an audience that failed to plan their retirement. This is an audience that planned it, executed it, discovered unforeseen problems, and is open to a course correction. They deserve to be addressed as the experienced, capable people they are.

The pitch is not "Arizona instead of Florida." It is "if Florida isn't what you thought it would be, here is what mountain retirement in Arizona looks like — and how the costs compare."

**The right outcome:** A retired couple from Naples who are paying $8,000/year in homeowner's insurance and haven't slept well through hurricane season in three years reads this and thinks *"Why didn't we look at Arizona?"*

### The Florida Insurance Crisis — The Central Pitch

Florida's homeowner's insurance market is in genuine crisis. Multiple major carriers have left the state. Citizens Property Insurance (the state insurer of last resort) has surged in enrollment and rates. In coastal areas, comprehensive coverage can cost $10,000–$18,000 per year, and some buyers cannot get coverage at any price.

For retirees on fixed incomes, an insurance bill that exceeds their property taxes is a meaningful budget crisis. This is the specific pain point that drives reverse migration from Florida, and it should be addressed directly.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Validate the original Florida decision.** They made a reasonable choice based on available information. The situation changed. This is not about them making a mistake.
- **Lead with the insurance angle.** It is the most tangible and immediate pain point for this audience.
- **Acknowledge Florida's genuine strengths.** No income tax, warm year-round, beaches, established retirement infrastructure. Don't pretend these aren't real.
- **Note what transfers.** The retirement lifestyle — outdoor living, community, accessible healthcare nearby (Prescott Valley, Phoenix) — is available in Arizona too.
- **Address the heat myth directly.** Many people think "Arizona" means Phoenix desert heat. Goswick Ranch at 6,000 feet is nothing like Sarasota summer humidity. This is a critical misconception to correct.

### DON'T
- Don't mock Florida or Florida retirees. Many are happy in Florida; this page is for the ones who aren't.
- Don't ignore Florida's income tax advantage. FL has no income tax; AZ has 2.5%. Be honest that FL is better on this metric.
- Don't oversell the insurance comparison — wildfire insurance in Arizona is a real consideration, and the audience has been burned (literally) by ignoring insurance risk.

### H2: The Insurance Situation — An Honest Comparison

Florida's homeowner's insurance market has reached crisis levels. Multiple major carriers have exited the state. Citizens Insurance — the state's insurer of last resort — has become a primary insurer for hundreds of thousands of Florida homeowners, with rates that continue to rise.

In coastal Southwest Florida after Hurricane Ian, annual premiums for modest homes in Naples and Fort Myers reached $15,000–$20,000. Some properties became uninsurable at any price.

Arizona's insurance market is functional. Wildfire risk exists in mountain terrain — and must be priced into your decision honestly. But the market has not collapsed, carriers have not exited, and premiums are not running at Florida coastal levels.

Get a quote from an Arizona-licensed insurer for any specific parcel. Then compare it to what you're paying now.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Arizona's summer heat like Florida's?**
No. Phoenix summer heat is dry and can exceed 115°F — comparable to Florida in intensity but different in character. Goswick Ranch at 4,400–6,000 feet is different from both. Daytime highs run in the 80s°F through summer, with dry air and no humidity.

**Does Arizona have the insurance problems Florida has?**
No. Arizona's homeowner's insurance market is functional. Wildfire risk exists in mountain terrain and should be evaluated for any specific property — but the state is not in the crisis conditions that have affected Florida's coastal market.

**Does Florida have a tax advantage over Arizona?**
Yes on income tax: Florida has none; Arizona's is 2.5% flat. This is approximately $2,000/year on $80,000 of income. The insurance and property tax savings at Goswick Ranch often exceed this for coastal Florida homeowners.

**Can I own Arizona property part-time?**
Yes. No occupancy requirement. Seasonal, part-time, or investment use all permitted.
