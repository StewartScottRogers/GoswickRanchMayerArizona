## Section 1: Right Outside Your Door

**H2:** Right Outside Your Door

### Trails — Prescott National Forest (Bradshaw District)

Goswick Ranch sits at the gateway to the Bradshaw Ranger District of Prescott National Forest. Poland Road itself climbs 5.9 miles from the ranch to **Five Corners** at 6,926 feet — the hub of the Bradshaw Mountain trail network.

**From Five Corners, you can access:**
- Hiking and equestrian trails into the Bradshaw Mountains
- Mountain biking routes
- BLM and State Trust Land trailheads
- Links to the Arizona Trail

Contact the **Bradshaw Ranger District:** (928) 443-8000

### OHV — Big Bug Mesa

**Big Bug Mesa OHV Area (FR 670)** — approximately 5 miles from Goswick Ranch.

- Managed by the Bradshaw Ranger District
- Suitable for ATVs, UTVs, dirt bikes, and 4WD vehicles
- Open riding on managed routes through the high desert

### Horseback Riding

The Prescott National Forest provides hundreds of miles of maintained equestrian trails accessible from the Poland Road corridor. **Foothills Ranch** (7250 S SR 69, Mayer; ~7 minutes from the gate) offers guided trail rides into the Bradshaws for guests and non-horse-owners.

### Hunting

Goswick Ranch falls within **Game Management Units 20A and 20B** (Yavapai County). The area supports:
- **Deer:** Mule deer; various rifle, archaic, and muzzleloader seasons
- **Elk:** Unit 20B has limited elk tags available through draw
- **Turkey, Javelina, Dove, Quail**
- **Mountain Lion and Black Bear** (units 20A/20B)

Current season dates: Arizona Game & Fish — https://www.azgfd.com/hunting/seasons/

### Birding

The Mayer / Poland Road corridor is on a significant spring migration corridor. Regular species include golden eagles, red-tailed hawks, great horned owls, Gambel's quail, hummingbirds (3–4 species), Western bluebirds, and multiple woodpecker species.

## Section 2: Prescott (27 Miles)

**H2:** Prescott — 27 Miles North (~35 minutes)

> Arizona's territorial capital. Historic downtown, genuine four seasons, 450+ miles of National Forest trails within the city limits.

**Highlights:**
- **Whiskey Row** (Montezuma St) — Arizona's oldest bar, The Palace Restaurant & Saloon (est. 1877); historic bars and shops
- **Courthouse Plaza** — one of America's top 10 great public spaces; free summer concerts, farmers market (Saturdays)
- **Prescott Frontier Days Rodeo** — World's Oldest Rodeo, running continuously since 1888 (late June–July 4th weekend)
- **Sharlot Hall Museum** — 4 acres, 11 exhibit buildings including the 1864 Governor's Mansion
- **Phippen Museum of Western Art** — Arizona Rancher and Cowboy Hall of Fame
- **Granite Mountain Wilderness** — only urban wilderness in Prescott NF
- **Prescott Airport (PRC)** — daily United Express flights to LAX and Denver; free long-term parking

**Practical services in Prescott:**
Trader Joe's, Safeway, Fry's, Sprouts, Costco, Lowe's, Home Depot, full medical (YRMC West hospital).

## Section 3: Verde Valley, Jerome & Sedona (45–65 Miles)

**H2:** Verde Valley Wine Trail, Jerome & Sedona — 45–65 Miles

### Verde Valley Wine Trail

The Verde Valley was designated an **American Viticultural Area (AVA) in 2021**. Wine country is a 45–55 minute drive from Goswick Ranch.

**Key wineries:**
| Winery | Location | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Page Springs Cellars | Oak Creek, Cornville | One of Arizona's most acclaimed — Rhône-style varietals |
| Alcantara Vineyards | Verde River | Live music, hay rides, horseback, kayaking, tubing |
| Javelina Leap Vineyard | Page Springs Rd | Boutique; strong reds |
| Caduceus Cellars / Merkin Vineyards | Jerome tasting room | Founded by Maynard James Keenan (Tool) |

**Route:** SR 69 south → I-17 north → Exit 287 (SR 260) toward Cottonwood. ~47 miles, ~48 minutes.

### Jerome

A National Historic Landmark, Jerome was a copper mining boomtown of 10,000+ at its peak; today 464 residents occupy the hillside Victorian buildings.

- Art galleries, restaurants, and boutiques in the historic downtown
- **Jerome State Historic Park** — Douglas Mansion (1916), open-pit mine overview, mining history
- **Jerome Grand Hotel** — Originally the 1927 United Verde Hospital; ghost tours available
- **Distance from Mayer:** ~40 miles, ~50 minutes

### Sedona

- **Distance from Mayer:** ~60 miles, ~62 minutes
- Spectacular red rock formations (Pink Schnebly Hill sandstone)
- Best accessible viewpoints: Cathedral Rock, Bell Rock, Airport Mesa, Chapel of the Holy Cross
- **Parking note:** Popular trailheads fill by 7:00 AM on weekends. Go on weekdays or arrive before 8 AM.
- Red Rock Pass: $5/day for Forest Service trailhead parking (America the Beautiful passes accepted)
