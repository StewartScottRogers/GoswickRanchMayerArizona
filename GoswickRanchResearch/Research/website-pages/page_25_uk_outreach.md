## What We're Trying to Do

## Critical Legal Considerations

**These must be addressed upfront. UK buyers will know to ask.**

### Visa and Stay Rules
- UK citizens can enter the US under the Visa Waiver Programme (ESTA) for **up to 90 days** per visit without a visa.
- For longer stays, a B-2 tourist visa can be obtained, potentially allowing up to **6 months** per visit.
- Owning US property does not confer any US immigration rights.
- For full-time relocation, a US immigration pathway would be required (investor visa, spousal, etc.).
- **Important framing:** Most UK buyers are in the holiday/investment category, not the full-relocation category.

### US Tax on UK Buyers
- UK citizens who own US property are subject to the same tax rules as Canadian buyers: FIRPTA on sale, US estate tax exposure on US-sited property.
- The US–UK tax treaty provides some relief, but cross-border professional advice is essential.
- UK buyers must also report US property income (if any) and any rental income on their UK tax return.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Be direct and factual.** British buyers respond poorly to American-style promotional copy. Low-key, specific, and data-oriented works better.
- **Acknowledge the visa limitation immediately.** Do not bury the 90-day/6-month reality — bring it up early and frame it accurately.
- **Speak to the investment case on its own merits.** Arizona land at $275,000 USD with $692/year in carrying costs is a genuinely compelling investment argument for a UK buyer holding dollars or diversifying out of pounds.
- **Note the dollar-denominated asset angle.** UK buyers with international portfolios understand currency diversification. USD assets in a low-tax jurisdiction are a portfolio argument, not just a lifestyle one.
- **Address the family connection if applicable.** Many UK buyers have children or grandchildren in US cities. The "visit base" framing resonates.

### DON'T
- Don't use American colloquialisms or sales language.
- Don't pitch this as a retirement home if the buyer cannot retire there full-time. Be accurate about the visa reality.
- Don't ignore FIRPTA and UK tax reporting obligations — UK buyers will ask.
- Don't assume they know where Arizona is relative to anything. Brief geography orientation is helpful.
