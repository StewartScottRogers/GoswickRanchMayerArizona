## Section 1: Wildfire

**H2:** Wildfire Risk and Defensible Space

> The 2017 Goodwin Fire burned 28,516 acres in the Big Bug Creek watershed immediately north of Mayer. The subsequent monsoon caused catastrophic flooding on July 19, 2017 — 109 homes damaged in under 4 hours. The Brady Fire (February 2025) burned 400 acres west of Mayer, triggering GO evacuations.
>
> Goswick Ranch is firmly in the Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI). Wildfire preparation is not optional — it is part of living here.

### Defensible Space Zones

**Zone 0 — Immediate Zone (0–5 feet):**
Use non-combustible materials (gravel, pavers, concrete); remove dead weeds, dry grass, debris; eliminate combustibles stored against the house.

**Zone 1 — Intermediate Zone (5–30 feet):**
- Outbuildings and propane tanks: 10 feet cleared to bare soil + 10-foot perimeter
- Remove low-hanging limbs (up to 6 feet from ground)
- Remove dead plants, grass, and debris; mow grasses short

**Zone 2 — Extended Zone (30–100 feet or to property line):**
- Space trees so crowns have 18+ feet between them
- Limb trees 6–10 feet from ground
- Remove dead vegetation and ladder fuels

**Free risk assessments:** Mayer Fire District will assess your property at no charge. Call **(928) 632-9534**.

### Fire Restriction Status

Fire restrictions change seasonally based on drought and fire danger conditions.

| Resource | Link |
|---|---|
| Current Yavapai County fire ban | https://www.yavapaiready.gov/FireBan |
| DFFM state fire restrictions | https://dffm.az.gov/fire-restrictions |
| Prescott National Forest alerts | https://www.fs.usda.gov/r03/prescott/alerts |

**Stage 1 restrictions:** No campfires outside developed rings; no open burning; no smoking while traveling through wildland.
**Stage 2 restrictions:** No campfires anywhere; no fireworks; stricter equipment and ignition rules.

### Firewise USA

Becoming a Firewise USA recognized community provides homeowners insurance discounts (5–15% from regional carriers) and qualifies the HOA for state fire prevention incentives.

Contact: Yavapai Firewise (PAWUIC) — (928) 277-8032 or yavapaifirewise.org

## Section 2: Evacuation

**H2:** Evacuation Planning

### Know Your Zone

Your evacuation zone determines when you will receive SET and GO alerts.

1. Visit **https://protect.genasys.com** and enter your address — your zone will be shown
2. Download the **Genasys Protect** app for real-time zone status on your phone

### Sign Up for Emergency Alerts

**AlertYavapai (Smart911/RAVE):**
- Text **alertYAVAPAI** to **78015**
- Or register online: https://www.smart911.com/smart911/ref/reg.action?pa=yavapaiaz
- Create a profile with your address and preferred contact method (text, call, email, or app)

> **Note:** Yavapai County switched from Everbridge to Smart911/RAVE in late 2022. If you registered under the old system, you must re-register.

### Evacuation Route from Goswick Ranch

1. Exit the community onto **E Poland Road**
2. Turn onto **SR 69**
3. **North on SR 69** toward Prescott Valley / Prescott (preferred)
4. Or south on SR 69 to I-17, then north toward Prescott or south toward Phoenix

**Critical:** SR 69 is the **only paved evacuation corridor** from the Mayer area. If SR 69 is compromised, there is no readily available alternative paved route. Do not wait if evacuation seems likely — early evacuation is safer.

**Real-time road conditions:** Call **511** or visit https://az511.gov

### Livestock Evacuation

If you have horses or large animals, register with the **L.A.S.E.R.** (Large Animal Shelter & Emergency Readiness) program. Contact Yavapai Firewise — (928) 277-8032.

## Section 3: Flood Risk

**H2:** Flood Risk — Big Bug Creek

> In any year following a significant wildfire in the Big Bug Creek watershed, the subsequent monsoon season carries elevated flash flood risk. Burned soil creates a hydrophobic layer that repels water instead of absorbing it — a modest monsoon storm can produce catastrophic flooding downstream.

### Real-Time Flood Monitoring

| Resource | Link |
|---|---|
| USGS Big Bug Creek gauge (site 09507980) | https://waterdata.usgs.gov/monitoring-location/09507980/ |
| Big Bug Creek at SR 69 (Yavapai County ALERT) | https://yavapaiaz.gov/ycflood/Weather/Weather-Stations-Gauges/ArtMID/6450/ArticleID/10997/3705-Big-Bug-Creek-at-SR69-YMBA3 |
| Yavapai County ALERT System (5-min updates) | https://gis.yavapaiaz.gov/alert/ |

### Flood Insurance

Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flooding. Properties in FEMA Zone AE (along Big Bug Creek) may be required to carry flood insurance with a federally backed mortgage. The standard 30-day waiting period may be waived for post-wildfire flooding from adjacent federal land.

Check your property: https://msc.fema.gov/portal/search

## Section 4: APS Power Outages

**H2:** Power Outages

APS has identified Mayer as subject to **Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS)** during extreme fire weather. If an outage is forecast:
- Stock 3+ days of essential medications
- Have backup power for medical equipment
- Stock water (especially if your well pump is electric)
- APS PSPS page: https://www.aps.com/en/Utility/Outage/Public-Safety-Power-Shutoff
- APS outage reporting: **(800) 253-9405**
