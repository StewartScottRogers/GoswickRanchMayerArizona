## Section 1: What Requires ARC Approval

**H2:** What Needs Approval

Under the Goswick Ranch CC&Rs and Arizona law (ARS §33-1817), modifications to your property's exterior or structures must be approved by the board before work begins.

**Generally requires approval:**
- New structures (barns, sheds, fences, corrals, guest houses, garages)
- Exterior paint color changes
- Additions to existing structures
- Significant landscaping changes visible from common areas or roads
- Fences and gates
- Driveways and road access modifications
- Solar panel installations visible from the street

**Generally does not require approval:**
- Interior modifications
- Like-for-like repairs using the same materials and color
- Routine maintenance and landscaping upkeep

> **When in doubt, ask first.** Submitting a request costs nothing and avoids the expense of undoing unapproved work. Contact the board before starting any project you are uncertain about.

### What to Include

A complete ARC submission includes:

1. **Property address and lot number**
2. **Description of the project** — what you're building or changing, materials, dimensions
3. **Proposed start and completion dates**
4. **Contractor name and contact** (if applicable; owner-built is fine too)
5. **Photos or drawings** — current condition and what the finished result will look like
6. **Confirmation** that you have reviewed the CC&Rs and design guidelines

## Section 3: Timeline and Process

**H2:** What to Expect

Under Arizona law (ARS §33-1817), unless the CC&Rs specify otherwise:
- The board has **60 days** to approve or deny a request after receiving a complete application
- If no response is given within 60 days, the modification is **deemed approved by default**
- The board may not deny requests in an arbitrary or capricious manner
- Approvals may include conditions (e.g., paint color modifications, specific materials)

**Typical process:**
1. Submit your request with all required information
2. Board reviews at the next regular meeting or via email vote
3. You receive written approval, denial, or request for additional information
4. Approved: begin work as described in the approval
5. Complete work substantially as approved; notify the board of completion
