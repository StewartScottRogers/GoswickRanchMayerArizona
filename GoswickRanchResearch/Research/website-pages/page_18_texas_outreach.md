## What We're Trying to Do

Texas is a unique pitch. Unlike New York or California, Texas has no state income tax — so the financial angle is narrower. The pitch here has two distinct drivers: **heat and property taxes.**

Texas summers are brutal. Phoenix heat is the punchline Arizonans use, but Houston and Dallas regularly hit 105°F+ with 90% humidity from June through September. Goswick Ranch at 6,000 feet in the Bradshaws is 80°F in July. For a retired Texan, that is not a small thing.

Texas also has some of the highest property taxes in the United States — 2–2.5% of assessed value with no cap on reassessment for new purchases. The financial case is real, even without the income tax angle.

**The right outcome:** A retired engineer from Houston, a retired rancher from San Antonio, a DFW couple who've been planning the "someday" move reads this and thinks *"This looks like Texas — but cooler. And the taxes are different."*

### The Ranch Culture Bridge

Texas has deep ranching heritage. Many Texas retirees have owned or worked land, know what 9 acres means, understand private well and propane infrastructure, and find horses and livestock practical rather than exotic.

For this audience, Goswick Ranch is not an introduction to rural life — it's a cooler, cheaper version of something they already understand.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Lead with the summer heat comparison.** It's the most visceral point of difference. A Houstonian who has spent 40 summers in humidity will understand "80°F in July" immediately.
- **Respect the ranch culture identity.** Texans with land experience will engage with the practical details — well water, propane, livestock, fencing. Don't over-explain these.
- **Acknowledge TX has no income tax.** Don't pretend this doesn't exist. The pitch shifts to property taxes and climate, and that's fine.
- **Use the property tax comparison prominently.** Texas property taxes are genuinely painful, especially in metro counties. This lands.
- **Mention AZ land values vs TX land values.** In many Texas metro areas, comparable acreage is far more expensive than Goswick Ranch.
- **Note driving distance.** Texas retirees drive. El Paso to Phoenix is 4 hours. San Antonio to Phoenix is 8 hours via I-10. Mention this.

### DON'T
- Don't oversell the income tax angle — TX has no income tax, and this audience knows it.
- Don't use anti-Texas language or imply they're escaping anything.
- Don't underestimate the ranching sophistication of this audience. They know more about land management than most.
- Don't ignore hurricane/flood buyers from the Gulf Coast — this is a specific motivated sub-audience.

### H2: The Summer Difference

Houston averages over 100 days per year above 90°F. Dallas is similar. Add humidity, and July in Houston is not a place where aging joints and outdoor plans coexist well.

At 4,400–6,000 feet in the Bradshaw Mountains, Goswick Ranch averages highs in the 80s°F through summer. The monsoon brings dramatic afternoon thunderstorms mid-June through September — intense, brief, and followed by cool air. It is nothing like Phoenix, and nothing like Houston.

Spring brings wildflowers. Fall turns the oaks. Winter is 40s–50s°F days with occasional snow and some of the darkest, most star-filled skies in the American West.

Phoenix is 75 miles south if you need the heat. Goswick Ranch is 6,000 feet up.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Arizona have lower property taxes than Texas?**
Yes. Arizona's effective property tax rate is among the lowest in the US. At Goswick Ranch, median property taxes run approximately $476/year. Texas's effective rate of 2–2.5% means a $300,000 Texas property carries $6,000–$7,500/year in taxes. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

**Does Texas have an income tax advantage over Arizona?**
Texas has no state income tax; Arizona's is 2.5% flat. On $80,000 in retirement income, that's approximately $2,000/year that Texas keeps in your pocket. The property tax savings in Arizona more than offset this for most buyers, but the net depends on your specific income and property values.

**Can I own Arizona property and live there part of the year?**
Yes. No occupancy requirement. Part-time, seasonal, or investment use all permitted.

**How hot does Goswick Ranch get in summer?**
Daytime highs are typically in the 80s°F at elevation. This compares to Phoenix at 110°F+ and Texas metros at 100–108°F with humidity.
