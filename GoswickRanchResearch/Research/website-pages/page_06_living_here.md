## Section 1: The Life

**H2:** Ranch Life at 6,000 Feet

> Goswick Ranch at nearly 6,000 feet in the Bradshaw Mountains is a fundamentally different experience from life in Phoenix or even Prescott. Residents who thrive here value independence, outdoor access, the freedom of acreage, and neighbors who respect their space.
>
> The honest picture: Goswick Ranch is not a convenience-first community. The gate is 1.5 miles from the highway. The nearest grocery store is in Mayer (1 mile) or Prescott Valley (13 miles). Amazon delivers, but you'll want propane tanks. You're trading commute access for space, quiet, and sky.

**What residents cite most:**
- The stars — Bortle Class 3–4, Milky Way visible on clear nights
- The quiet — no traffic noise, no neighbors crowding your view
- The wildlife — deer, elk, bears, eagles as daily companions
- The seasons — spring wildflowers, summer monsoons, fall oak color, occasional winter snow
- The horses — hundreds of miles of National Forest trails accessible from the property

## Section 2: The Four Seasons

**H2:** Four Seasons at Elevation

| Season | Character |
|---|---|
| **Winter** | Daytime 40s–50s°F; occasional snow (Prescott averages ~10 in/year); quiet dormancy; brilliant starry nights |
| **Spring** | Warming; wildflowers February–April (Mexican gold poppy, desert marigold, lupine); oaks leafing out; nesting birds returning |
| **Summer** | Daytime highs typically 80s°F (vs. Phoenix 110°F+); monsoon season mid-June to September 30 |
| **Fall** | September–November; oak, cottonwood, and maple color; crisp mornings; hunting season; many residents' favorite season |

### Monsoon Season

> July and August bring the monsoon — up to half the year's precipitation in 8 weeks. Storms form over the Black Hills and Bradshaws, triggering on the high terrain first. Towering thunderheads, vivid lightning, sudden cool rain after dry heat, and the smell of desert rain on hot rock. It is one of the defining experiences of living in the central Arizona highlands.

**Flood awareness:** Big Bug Creek can rise quickly during monsoon, especially following any wildfire in the watershed. See the Emergency Preparedness page for flood monitoring resources.

## Section 3: Horses, Livestock, and Land Use

**H2:** What You Can Do with Your Land

Goswick Ranch is zoned **RCU-2A (Rural Community Unincorporated)** with a **9-acre minimum lot size** established by the CC&Rs (county minimum is 2 acres). This zoning enables genuine rural land use.

### Livestock and Animals

Farm animals are permitted for the convenience and pleasure of lot occupants under Yavapai County's Allowed Animal Chart (Sec. 501E). Key rules:

- **Horses:** Explicitly allowed. RCU-2A zoning supports equestrian properties.
- **Cattle, goats, and other large livestock:** Permitted per the Allowed Animal Chart acreage breakdown
- **Chickens:** Limited to 8 per lot; roosters are prohibited in residential zones
- **Dogs and cats:** Standard residential pet rules apply

For the complete animal chart by lot acreage: contact Yavapai County Development Services at (928) 771-3214.

### Horse Culture Here

Trail riding in Prescott National Forest (Bradshaw District) provides hundreds of miles of maintained equestrian trails — accessible directly from the Poland Road corridor. Foothills Ranch (7250 S SR 69, Mayer; 5 miles away) offers guided trail rides into the Bradshaws.

**Large-animal veterinarians serving Mayer:**
- Los Caballos Veterinary Service — (928) 636-6644; 24/7 emergency
- Prescott Animal Hospital Equine Center — (928) 776-7669

**Feed and ranch supply:**
- Olsen's Grain, Dewey/Humboldt (~15 miles) — (928) 632-5233; hay delivery available
- Olsen's Grain, Prescott — (928) 445-1664

### Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)

Yavapai County adopted new ADU standards November 19, 2025:
- **One attached + one detached ADU** permitted on single-family lots
- Maximum size: 1,000 sq ft
- Building permit and septic/water review required
- **Important:** The Goswick Ranch CC&Rs may further restrict ADUs — verify with the board before planning.

### Short-Term Rentals

Arizona state law allows STRs in unincorporated areas, but:
- STRs in unincorporated Yavapai County **do not allow events** (weddings, parties, commercial activities)
- The Goswick Ranch CC&Rs may additionally restrict STRs — confirm with the board.

### Camping on Your Property

Camping is allowed on 2-acre+ lots in RCU-2A zoning: **10 days at a time, 3 times per year, with 30 days between each stay.**

## Section 4: Open Range

**H2:** Open Range — Know Before You Drive

Arizona is a **"fence out" state**. If you want to keep livestock off your property, you must build a lawful fence. Livestock owners bear no general liability for animals on the open range.

**Critical:** If you hit and injure livestock on E Poland Rd or SR 69, **you (the driver) may be liable to the livestock owner** under Arizona open range law (ARS §3-1426). This is the law throughout unincorporated Yavapai County. Drive Poland Road and SR 69 with awareness, especially at dawn and dusk.

**Lawful fence spec** (to exclude livestock per ARS §3-1426):
- Posts no more than 30 feet apart, firmly in the ground
- At least 4 strands of barbed wire, tightly stretched
- Wire heights: top at 50 inches; others at 38, 28, and 18 inches

## Section 5: Utilities

**H2:** Utilities

| Utility | What You Have |
|---|---|
| Water | **Individual private well** — each lot has its own well. No community water system. |
| Sewer | **Individual septic system** — no community sewer. Shallow bedrock common in granite terrain may affect septic design. |
| Electricity | **APS (Arizona Public Service)** — serves the Mayer / Poland Junction corridor. |
| Gas | **Propane** — no natural gas line. Four providers serve the area (see Resident Resources page). |
| Internet | **Starlink** is available and confirmed at ZIP 86333 (50–180 Mbps, 20–60ms latency). Cellular: Verizon (77%), AT&T (76%), T-Mobile (73%) — test on-site at elevation. |
| Trash | Contact the board for current trash collection provider. |

### Well Water and Arsenic

> **Water quality notice:** 43.6% of Yavapai County groundwater samples exceed the EPA's 10 ppb arsenic limit. The Big Bug Mining District encompasses Goswick Ranch and has documented metal leaching risk from historic mining activity.
>
> **Recommendation:** Test your private well every 5 years for arsenic, lead, nitrates, and mining-related metals. ADHR well water testing information: https://www.azdhs.gov/wellwater
