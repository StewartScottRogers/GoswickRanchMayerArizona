## What We're Trying to Do

The DC/Mid-Atlantic audience is distinct from NYC in important ways. There is a large population of federal government retirees, military retirees, and contractors with pensions and defined benefit income — people who have predictable fixed income and are doing very specific math on where to make it go further. There is also a large professional class (law, lobbying, consulting, healthcare) with a different kind of equity.

The financial case is strong. Maryland and DC have high income taxes. Virginia is slightly lower but still above Arizona. All three have high property taxes compared to Yavapai County.

The cultural case is also specific: after 30 years of DC commuting, bureaucracy, and density, "no one is in your business here" lands as a genuine promise, not just a sales line.

**The right outcome:** A retired federal employee from Bethesda, a retired Navy officer from Virginia Beach, a retired doctor from the Philadelphia suburbs reads this and thinks *"This is where the pension goes further. Let's see it."*

### The Federal Retirement Angle

Federal employees on FERS or CSRS pensions have predictable monthly income. This audience is doing very precise math on what their pension buys in different states. Arizona's low property taxes, flat income tax, and no estate tax are each meaningful line items.

Arizona does not tax military retirement pay. This is a significant point for military retirees.

## Cultural Sensitivities

### DO
- **Name the DC dynamic specifically.** "After 30 years of navigating federal bureaucracy, you've earned somewhere that doesn't require it" is a real statement, not a platitude.
- **Address the military audience directly.** Arizona does not tax military retirement pay. This should be stated plainly.
- **Respect the professional identity.** DC-area retirees are educated, analytical, and will do thorough research. Provide data; don't over-sell.
- **Acknowledge Philadelphia's identity separately.** Philly has its own working-class culture distinct from DC professionals. The value and land story works for this audience; the bureaucracy angle doesn't land the same way.
- **Note proximity to major airports.** DC to Phoenix is a direct flight. For people who may split time between Arizona and DC, this matters.

### DON'T
- Don't conflate DC culture with the rest of the Mid-Atlantic. Baltimore, Philadelphia, and Hampton Roads are distinct.
- Don't oversell the anti-government angle. Many DC retirees are proud of their federal careers.
- Don't ignore the Maryland county income tax. It's a real additional burden on top of state tax.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Arizona tax military retirement pay?**
No. Arizona does not tax military retirement income. Maryland taxes it fully; Virginia offers partial exemptions. For military retirees, this is a meaningful annual savings.

**How do Maryland and DC income taxes compare to Arizona?**
Maryland's combined state and county income tax can reach 8.95%. DC's top rate is 10.75%. Arizona's rate is 2.5% flat. On $80,000 in retirement income, the difference between MD and AZ can be $4,000–$5,000/year.

**Can I own the property part-time?**
Yes. No occupancy requirement. Seasonal, part-time, or investment use all permitted.

**What are property taxes at Goswick Ranch?**
Approximately $476/year median on improved parcels. Compare to Montgomery County, MD at ~$5,500/year or Fairfax County, VA at ~$7,000/year.
