## What We're Trying to Do

Goswick Ranch is an authentic, working Arizona ranching community. We are NOT trying to market it as a resort, a lifestyle brand, or a manufactured retirement community. The goal is to present this place honestly, in a voice and with reference points that land naturally for someone who has spent their life in or around New York City.

This is a **soft touch**. We are not selling. We are introducing. If the place is right for them, the facts will do the work.

**The right outcome:** A retired firefighter from Staten Island, a teacher from Westchester, a tradesman from Bergen County NJ — someone who worked hard their whole life, paid high taxes, lived in a crowded place — reads this page and thinks *"I didn't know this existed. Tell me more."*

## Target Audience: NYC Metro Retirees

### Who We're Talking To

The NYC metropolitan area includes:
- The five boroughs (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island)
- Long Island (Nassau and Suffolk Counties)
- Northern New Jersey (Bergen, Essex, Hudson, Morris, Passaic, Union)
- Connecticut (Fairfield, New Haven corridors)
- Hudson Valley / Westchester

This is not a monolithic audience. But retirement-age buyers from this region tend to share common pressures:

| Pressure | NYC Metro Reality |
|---|---|
| Property taxes | Among the highest in the US: NJ avg ~$9,000/yr; NYC co-ops often $1,000–$3,000/month in maintenance fees alone |
| State income tax | NY: up to 10.9%; NJ: up to 10.75%; CT: up to 6.99% |
| Cost of living | NYC is consistently top 3–5 most expensive US cities |
| Weather | Cold, grey winters; humid summers; not mild |
| Density | Even "suburban" NJ has density NYC residents would recognize |
| Commute | Many spent 30–40 years commuting 60–90 min each way |

**These are not complaints about NYC culture. They are facts that, at retirement, become calculus.**

### Who Is NOT the Audience Here

- Trend-seekers looking for the next "it" place
- People who want resort amenities or concierge services
- Buyers expecting urban-style conveniences on-site
- People unfamiliar with rural tradeoffs (Goswick Ranch is honest about this on the Living Here page)

## Cultural Sensitivities to Navigate

### DO

- **Acknowledge New York identity as an asset.** People who thrive in NYC for 30–40 years are tough, resourceful, and self-reliant. Those are the same qualities that work in a rural Arizona community. Name this.
- **Use authentic language.** Goswick Ranch is not a "luxury retreat." It is a real working-ranch community. NYC residents are extraordinarily good at detecting BS — and extraordinarily loyal when they find something real.
- **Lead with contrast, not escape.** The framing should be *"this is what's here"* not *"leave that behind."* Retirement is not running away. It's choosing something different.
- **Respect working-class roots.** Much of the NYC metro retirement pool (outer-borough, NJ suburbs, Long Island) has deep working-class identity. A ranching community with horses, fencing, and land management is not foreign — it's familiar in a different key.
- **Use numbers they understand.** They know what $9,000/yr in NJ property taxes feels like. They know what $216/yr HOA dues means by comparison. Let the math land without editorializing.

### DON'T

- Don't frame NYC as something to escape from. That reads as condescending.
- Don't use "wide open spaces" or "freedom" language that implies NYC is a cage. It isn't — it's just a different life.
- Don't assume everyone from NYC is wealthy Manhattan. The outer boroughs and NJ/CT suburbs are diverse in income and background.
- Don't oversell the silence as a universal good. Urban noise is part of some people's identity. The pitch is *"here's what's different"* — not *"here's what you've been missing."*
- Don't use cowboy clichés or over-romanticize the ranch aesthetic. Present facts. The romance will land on its own.
- Don't ignore the real tradeoffs. NYC residents will research. Hiding the arsenic water issue, the distance from a hospital, or the propane situation will destroy credibility.

Most people who find Goswick Ranch weren't looking for Arizona. They were looking for a way out of a cost structure that stopped making sense after the kids were grown and the mortgage was paid off.

If you've spent your life in or around New York — and you've done the math on property taxes, state income tax, and what another twenty years of maintenance fees looks like — you already know what we're about to say.

Goswick Ranch is a private gated community in the Bradshaw Mountains of central Arizona. It is not a resort. There is no golf course, no clubhouse, no lifestyle director. There are 25–35 private lots with a 9-acre minimum, horses and livestock permitted, a gated private road, and a volunteer board of property owners who manage the association themselves.

**The HOA fee is $216 a year.**

You can live here full-time, part-time, or hold the property as an investment. There is no occupancy requirement.

That's not for everyone. For some people, it's exactly enough.

### H2: New York vs. Arizona: The Cost Comparison

The numbers are worth putting side by side. These are estimates for illustration — your situation will vary. Consult a tax advisor for your specific circumstances.

| Item | New York / New Jersey | Goswick Ranch / Arizona |
|---|---|---|
| Annual property taxes | NJ median: ~$9,000/yr; NYC co-op maintenance often $12,000–$36,000/yr | **~$476/yr median** |
| Annual HOA or maintenance fee | NJ/NYC HOAs: often $3,600–$18,000/yr | **$216/year** |
| State income tax | NY: up to 10.9%; NJ: up to 10.75%; CT: up to 6.99% | **2.5% flat** |
| Estate tax | NY estate tax applies above $7.16M (with a "cliff" provision) | **No Arizona estate tax** |
| Social Security taxation | Taxed at state level in some configurations | **Not taxed in Arizona** |

#### H3: What That Adds Up To

On a combined retirement income of $80,000, the gap between New Jersey and Arizona taxation can exceed $7,000–$10,000 per year — before accounting for property tax savings. Over a 15-year retirement, that difference compounds.

This is not an argument to move. It is a number worth knowing.

### H2: Part-Time, Full-Time, or Investment — No Occupancy Requirement

There is no rule at Goswick Ranch that requires you to live here full-time. Owners may use the property seasonally, part-time, or hold it purely as an investment.

Some buyers purchase land and build on it later, using the parcel as a future retirement base while maintaining their Northeast residence. Some come out for a portion of the year — warm months or cool months, depending on preference. All of these are valid uses. The $216/year HOA applies to all of them.

### H2: What You're Getting for the Money

#### H3: Nine Acres in the Bradshaw Mountains

The 9-acre minimum lot size is not a county standard — it's a community covenant set in the CC&Rs, well above Yavapai County's 2-acre baseline. On a 9-acre lot in the Bradshaws, your nearest neighbor is not visible from your property line. The gate is on Poland Road. The noise is wildlife.

Active listings have ranged from $275,000 for vacant 9-acre land to $750,000 for improved ranch properties with existing structures. Property taxes on most parcels run under $500 a year. Some vacant land parcels have carried annual property taxes as low as $5.

#### H3: Four Seasons Without Five Months of Winter

Goswick Ranch is not the Arizona of Phoenix postcards. At 4,400–6,000 feet, the climate has genuine seasons:

- **Spring:** Wildflower season across the Bradshaw foothills — Mexican gold poppy, lupine, desert marigold. Oaks leafing out. Nesting birds returning.
- **Summer:** Daytime highs typically in the 80s°F while Phoenix sits at 110°F+. Monsoon season mid-June through September — dramatic afternoon thunderstorms, vivid lightning, the smell of desert rain on hot rock.
- **Fall:** Oak, cottonwood, and maple color. Crisp mornings. Many residents' favorite season.
- **Winter:** 40s–50s°F in the day with occasional snow at elevation. Clear, cold nights with the Milky Way overhead. Bortle Class 3–4 dark skies — something most New Yorkers haven't seen since childhood.

If you know what New England autumn looks like, the Bradshaw fall is its own version of that. Different palette. Same instinct.

### H2: How to Get Here — Planning a Visit from New York

Before you decide anything, come see it once.

**By air:**
Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX) is 74 miles from Goswick Ranch — approximately a 70-minute drive north via I-17 and SR 69. Nonstop flights from JFK and Newark (EWR) to Phoenix run approximately 5–5.5 hours. Multiple airlines serve this route daily.

From the airport: take I-17 North to Exit 262 (Cordes Junction / SR 69), then SR 69 East approximately 12 miles to Mayer. E Poland Road is 1.5 miles south of the town center.

**By car:**
From the New York area, the drive is approximately 2,400 miles — typically a 3–4 day trip via I-78 W / I-76 W to I-70 W through St. Louis, then I-44 W to I-40 W through Albuquerque, then I-17 S to SR 69. Many buyers make this as a scouting trip, stopping in Prescott for a night before driving the SR 69 corridor.

**Where to stay:**
Prescott, AZ (27 miles from the ranch) has solid lodging options and a walkable historic downtown. It makes a good base for exploring the area over a long weekend.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to retire in Arizona vs. New York?**

Arizona has a 2.5% flat state income tax (vs. New York's up to 10.9%), no estate tax, and does not tax Social Security income. Median property taxes at Goswick Ranch are approximately $476/year vs. New Jersey's median of ~$9,000/year. For retirees on fixed income, the difference is significant over a 10–20 year retirement horizon. Consult a tax advisor for your specific situation.

**Can I own property in Arizona and only live there part of the year?**

Yes. Goswick Ranch has no full-time occupancy requirement. Owners may live here part-time, seasonally, or hold the property as an investment.

**What is the HOA fee at Goswick Ranch?**

$216 per year ($18/month). This covers maintenance of the private internal road network and common association expenses. There is no management company — the HOA is self-managed by a volunteer board of property owners.

**How far is Goswick Ranch from New York?**

Approximately 2,400 miles by road; 5–5.5 hours nonstop by air from JFK or EWR to Phoenix Sky Harbor, then 70 minutes north by car.

**What are the lot sizes?**

The CC&Rs require a minimum of 9 acres — well above Yavapai County's 2-acre baseline. Most parcels are 9–38 acres.
