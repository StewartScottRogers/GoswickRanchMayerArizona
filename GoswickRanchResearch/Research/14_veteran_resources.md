*Research date: 2026-04-03*

## Overview

Goswick Ranch sits approximately 30 miles south of Prescott along AZ-69, placing residents within practical driving distance of a major VA Medical Center and a network of veteran support organizations. Yavapai County has a significant veteran population and correspondingly robust local resources. This file documents everything from VA hospitals to state tax exemptions.

## 2. Telehealth Options for Rural Veterans

Rural veterans at Goswick Ranch face the same barrier as other remote communities: driving 30+ miles for routine appointments. VA telehealth programs are designed specifically for this situation.

### 2a. VA Video Connect

VA's secure videoconferencing app for private video visits with VA providers from home or anywhere with internet. Over 10 million video visits were completed in FY 2025. Services available via VA Video Connect include:
- Primary care
- Mental health (PTSD, depression, anxiety)
- Dermatology
- Neurology
- Pulmonology
- Infectious disease
- Nutrition counseling
- Social work

**Requirements:** Enrolled in VA health care, an internet-connected device, and a VA Video Connect appointment scheduled through your care team.

**Setup guide:** [How to Use VA Video Connect — My HealtheVet](https://www.myhealth.va.gov/mhv-portal-web/web/myhealthevet/ss20200320-va-video-connect)

### 2b. My HealtheVet Patient Portal

The VA's online portal for managing prescriptions, appointments, medical records, and secure messaging with care teams.
**URL:** [myhealth.va.gov](https://www.myhealth.va.gov)

### 2c. ATLAS Sites (Accessing Telehealth through Local Area Stations)

ATLAS provides private, equipped telehealth stations in community locations (libraries, American Legion posts, etc.) for veterans without reliable home internet. An on-site attendant helps with technology but is not present during the appointment.

- One known Arizona ATLAS site: **American Legion Post 12, Wickenburg, AZ** (approximately 55 miles southwest of Mayer)
- VA Northern Arizona is part of the ATLAS network; check for any new sites near Prescott
- **To find ATLAS locations:** Visit [telehealth.va.gov/atlas](https://telehealth.va.gov/atlas) and search by ZIP code (86333)
- **ATLAS Help Desk:** 833-VA-ATLAS (833-822-8527), Mon–Fri 8 a.m.–8 p.m. ET

### 2d. VA MISSION Act — Community Care

Under the MISSION Act, veterans may be eligible for care from non-VA providers in the community (paid by VA) when:
- VA cannot offer a primary or mental health appointment within **20 days**
- VA cannot offer a specialty care appointment within **28 days**
- The nearest VA primary/mental health location is more than **30 minutes** drive
- The nearest VA specialty care location is more than **60 minutes** drive

Goswick Ranch residents are approximately 30 miles (roughly 40–45 minutes) from the Prescott VAMC, which may qualify some veterans for community care for certain specialty services. Discuss eligibility with your VA care team.

**Sources:** [VA Telehealth Services](https://telehealth.va.gov/) | [VA MISSION Act overview](https://vamissionact.com/) | [VA News — Telehealth Rural Veterans](https://news.va.gov/130255/telehealth-virtual-tools-deliver-rural-veterans/)

## 5. Arizona State Veteran Benefits

### 5a. Property Tax Exemption

Arizona law (ARS § 42-11111) exempts a portion or all of the assessed property value from taxation for veterans with qualifying service-connected or nonservice-connected disabilities.

**Key rules:**
- Veteran must be an Arizona resident and the property must be their primary residence
- Income limitations apply (Social Security, military pensions, and VA disability payments are **excluded** from income calculation)
- Disability rating from the VA determines the percentage of exemption

| Disability Category | Exemption |
|---|---|
| 1%–99% service-connected disability | Partial exemption based on disability % × allowable amount |
| 1%–100% nonservice-connected disability | Partial exemption based on disability % × allowable amount |
| 100% service-connected disability | Full exemption (property fully exempt from taxation) |

**2026 Tax Year exemption amount:** Up to **$4,873** in assessed limited property value deducted
*(For TY 2025, the amount was $4,476)*

**Application process (Yavapai County):**
- File an affidavit with the **Yavapai County Assessor's Office** when initially claiming
- Provide VA award letter stating disability percentage
- Applications for each tax year due by **February 28**
- Income documentation required

**Yavapai County Assessor's Office:**
- Address: 1015 Fair Street, Prescott, AZ 86305
- Phone: 928-771-3220
- Fax: 928-771-3181

**Sources:** [ARS § 42-11111](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/42/11111.htm) | [National Tax Reports — AZ](https://nationaltaxreports.com/property-tax-exemption-for-veterans-in-arizona/) | [Yavapai County Assessor](https://www.yavapaiaz.gov/Mapping-and-Properties/Assessors-Office)

### 5b. Hunting and Fishing License Benefits

Arizona Game and Fish offers three license tiers for veterans who are Arizona residents for at least one year:

### 5c. Veteran License Plates

Arizona offers multiple veteran and military specialty license plates through ADOT/MVD:
- **Cost:** $25 initial purchase fee + $25 annual renewal (personalization renewal fee waived by state statute for standard Veteran plates, plus all applicable vehicle licensing and registration fees may also be waived)
- **Revenue:** $17 of the $25 fee goes to the Arizona Veterans' Donation Fund, which supports health care, education, memorial cemeteries, and the State Veteran's Home
- **Proof required:** DD214, DD215, DD-2A, DD-2 (retired/reserve), DD-1173, or a statement of honorable service from VA or ADVS
- **Who can get one:** Also available to spouse, parent, child, sibling of an eligible veteran who holds the plate

**Veteran Driver's License designation:** Veterans can have a "Veteran" designation added to their Arizona driver's license, CDL, or ID card.

**CDL skills test waiver:** Veterans applying for an Arizona CDL may request a waiver of the skills test if they meet specific requirements.

**Source:** [DVS — Veteran License Plates](https://dvs.az.gov/resources/veteran-license-plates) | [ADOT Veterans page](https://azdot.gov/mvd/veterans)

## 8. VA Home Loan Benefits (Relevant to Goswick Ranch Buyers)

VA home loans are one of the most significant benefits for veteran home buyers and are directly relevant to anyone purchasing at Goswick Ranch.

### 8a. Core VA Loan Benefits

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **No down payment** | $0 down payment required (with full entitlement) |
| **No PMI** | No private mortgage insurance ever |
| **No loan limits** | Veteran borrowers with full entitlement are not restricted by VA county loan limits |
| **Competitive rates** | Historically lower than conventional rates |
| **2025 AZ loan limit reference** | $806,500 (used by lenders without full entitlement) |
| **Lifetime benefit** | Can be used multiple times |

### 8b. Funding Fee Exemption for Disabled Veterans

Veterans receiving VA disability compensation (any rating — 10% through 100%) are **exempt from the VA funding fee**, which is normally 1.25%–3.3% of the loan amount. This is a significant upfront cost savings.

### 8c. Rural Property Eligibility

- **No acreage limit** from VA itself; individual lenders may set their own limits
- Property must be residential in character and in reasonable condition
- VA does not restrict rural properties or large lots — Goswick Ranch parcels are eligible
- **Farm properties:** VA-guaranteed loans may include a home on a farm. The farm portion is not guaranteed, but the home and reasonable lot are. The property must be primarily residential.
- **Manufactured homes on owned land:** Eligible if built after June 15, 1976 (HUD Code), permanently affixed to an owned foundation, and meeting VA/local standards. The veteran must own the land — not leased land or a park.

### 8d. Specially Adapted Housing (SAH) Grant

For veterans with severe service-connected disabilities who need to build or modify a home:

| Grant | FY 2026 Max Amount | Eligibility |
|---|---|---|
| SAH (Specially Adapted Housing) | $126,526 | Severe mobility disabilities (wheelchair-level), blindness, limb loss |
| SHA (Special Housing Adaptation) | Lower amount | Less severe mobility/adaptation needs |

- Can be used up to **6 times** over a lifetime
- Apply via VA Form 26-4555
- **More info:** [VA Disability Housing Grants](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/disability-housing-grants/)

### 8e. How to Start

- **VA loan eligibility:** [va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/eligibility](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/eligibility/)
- **Certificate of Eligibility (COE):** Apply online through VA.gov or ask any VA-approved lender to pull it
- **VA loan guides for Arizona:** Multiple lenders operate in Yavapai County; Goswick Ranch is in a rural area well-suited to VA financing

**Sources:** [VA Home Loans](https://www.benefits.va.gov/homeloans/) | [VA Funding Fee](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/funding-fee-and-closing-costs/) | [Acreage limits](https://mortgagesolutions.net/2023/04/24/acreage-limits-va-home-loans/)

### 9f. USDA Rural Development Loans

USDA Section 502 Direct and Guaranteed loans are available for low-to-moderate income rural buyers regardless of military status. Veterans can use a VA loan instead of (or sometimes alongside consulting with) a USDA loan. Key differences:
- USDA requires income ≤ 115% of area median income; VA has no income cap
- USDA requires the property be in a USDA-designated rural area (Mayer 86333 qualifies)
- VA loan is generally superior for veterans with eligibility due to no income limits and no USDA annual fee

**USDA Arizona office:** [rd.usda.gov/az](https://www.rd.usda.gov/az)
