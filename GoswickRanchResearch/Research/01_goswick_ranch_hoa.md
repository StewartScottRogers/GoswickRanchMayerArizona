## Origins: The Historic Goswick Ranch

Multiple real estate listings state that subdivision lots are **"originally part of the historic Goswick Ranch"** — the modern HOA subdivision was carved from an actual working cattle ranch. The property includes references to "buildings, barns, and homes of the original ranch."

### The Goswick Family in Yavapai County

The Goswick family has deep, multi-generational roots in Yavapai County:

| Name | Born | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| William Isaac Goswick | 1836, TN | Earliest AZ ancestor; mail carrier Camp Verde–Payson route 1884–1914 |
| George Thomas Goswick | 1859, MO | Son of William Isaac; lived in Humboldt, Yavapai Co. |
| Charles "Giles" Goswick | 1888, TX | Government lion hunter; operated in Bradshaw Mountains area |
| George Allen Goswick | 1919, Camp Verde | **Confirmed in 1930 census in Mayer, Yavapai County** — strongest link to the ranch |

**Mark Goswick Cattle Co** is a documented active business at Bloody Basin Rd, Mayer, AZ 86333 (phone: 928-632-7730; ~$84,000 annual sales). Mark Goswick almost certainly descends from this same family line. The Goswick Cattle Co. also held BLM mining claim "SPANISH #8" (placer, T.12N R.1E Sec.5, AMC102929) — showing the combined ranching/mining identity typical of Bradshaw Mountain families.

**The original ranch homestead** is likely 5905 S Goswick Ranch Rd: built 1948/1949, 1,435–1,478 sq ft, 82.36-acre lot, not currently for sale. Zillow Zestimate ~$330,900.

## Zoning & Development Standards

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Zoning | RCU-2A (Rural Community Unincorporated — county baseline 2-acre minimum) |
| HOA minimum lot size | **9 acres** (CC&R restriction — much more restrictive than county minimum) |
| Minimum home size | 1,500 sq ft |
| Construction type | Site-built only (no manufactured/mobile homes) |
| Livestock | Permitted under RCU-2A, subject to Allowed Animal Chart (Sec. 501E, Yavapai Co. Ordinance) |
| Horses | Explicitly allowed under RCU-2A; equestrian community |
| Camping | Allowed on 2-acre+ lots, 10 days at a time, 3x/year, 30 days between stays |
| ADUs (Accessory Dwelling Units) | One attached + one detached permitted under new Yavapai County standards (adopted Nov 2025, max 1,000 sq ft); private CC&Rs may further restrict |
| Short-term rentals | State law allows in unincorporated areas; Goswick CC&Rs may further restrict — confirm with board |

### Patented Land Note
Several listings describe lots as "patented land" — meaning federal land converted to private ownership through the historic patent process (homestead, mining, etc.). Patented mining claims are common in the Bradshaw Mountains area.

## Lot Configuration & Internal Roads

**From the Yavapai County parcel map and listing research:**
- Approximately **25–35 individual parcels** in the subdivision
- Lot shapes are irregular — terrain-adapted, not a grid layout
- Main road: S Goswick Ranch Rd, plus internal roads: S Hawk Mountain Trail, S Miners Pick Rd, E Vaca Bonita Trail/Rd, Bullhorn Rd, E Pearl Mountain Rd, **E Elizabeth Mine Rd** (mining-heritage name)
- Internal roads are **private, gated, subdivision-maintained** ("Private Maintained Road, Subdivision Maintained Road" per listing data)
- Road maintenance is HOA responsibility per ARS §33-1818

**Gate access:** Physical lockbox/key system (not electronic keypad). Real estate agents obtain key from lockbox for showings. This affects visitor access, deliveries (FedEx, UPS, Amazon), and emergency services.

**Directions:** From AZ-69, turn west onto **E Poland Rd** (also signed as Poland Junction Rd) — this is the same road that climbs all the way up through the Bradshaws to Five Corners at 6,926 ft. Travel ~1.5 miles on Poland Rd; look for the Goswick Ranch sign. Gate accessed via lockbox.

| Utility | Status |
|---------|--------|
| Water | **Individual private wells** — no community water system. Lots with wells noted in listings: "private well already on property," "well + 3,600-gallon tank." |
| Sewer | **Individual septic systems** — shallow bedrock common in granite terrain may affect septic design |
| Electricity | **APS (Arizona Public Service)** — confirmed serving Mayer and Poland Junction corridor |
| Gas | **Propane** — no natural gas line. Four confirmed providers: Ferrellgas (928-445-3940), AmeriGas (800-263-7442), John Graves Propane (928-567-2425), Gilligan's Spring Valley 76 (928-632-4139). See `06_utilities_services.md`. |
| Internet | **Starlink** confirmed available at ZIP 86333 (50–180 Mbps, 20–60ms latency; fully capable for remote work). Cell coverage: Verizon (77.1%), AT&T (76.1%), T-Mobile (73.2%) citywide — on-site testing essential at 6,000 ft. |
| Trash | Not identified — board input needed |

**ARSENIC WARNING:** 43.6% of Yavapai County groundwater samples exceed the EPA's 10 ppb arsenic limit (NCBI study). The Big Bug Mining District (which encompasses Goswick Ranch) has documented mining activity that increases metal leaching risk. Private well owners should test every 5 years for arsenic, lead, nitrates, and mining-related metals. ADHR well water testing info: https://www.azdhs.gov/wellwater

**Water regulation:** Goswick Ranch appears to be **outside the Prescott Active Management Area** (Prescott AMA). Exempt domestic wells (<35 gpm) require only a Notice of Intention to Drill (NOI) filing with ADWR — no permit or ongoing monitoring required. To research specific wells: https://azwatermaps.azwater.gov/wellreg/
