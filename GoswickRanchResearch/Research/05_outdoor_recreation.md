### OHV / ATV Trails

**Big Bug Mesa OHV Trail System — ~5 miles / 10–15 min from Goswick Ranch**
- Located in Prescott National Forest (Bradshaw District), northeastern slopes of the Bradshaw Mountains
- Big Bug Mesa Trail: 5-mile dirt/gravel, multi-directional, ATV/OHV-rated (max 72" width)
- Big Bug Mesa Semi-Loop: 1.5-mile maintained loop around the mesa
- Dusty, rocky surface; Ponderosa pine forest; great overlook of the Bradshaws
- Trailforks: https://www.trailforks.com/trails/big-bug-mesa/
- TrailsOffroad: https://www.trailsoffroad.com/US/arizona/trails/4219-big-bug-mesa
- **Note:** The June 2016 Big Bug Mesa fire affected parts of the trail network; downed trees reported on some sections; Bradshaw Ranger District maintains clearance

### Five Corners / Five Points — The Hub of Poland Road

The **top of Poland Road** terminates at a junction locally called **"Five Corners"** (also "Five Points") — where **five roads converge** on the high plateau above Mayer. This is the primary staging area for Big Bug Mesa exploration and a key navigation landmark for anyone driving Poland Road.

**How to reach Five Corners:**

| Route | From | Distance | Notes |
|-------|------|----------|-------|
| **Poland Road** (CR 58 / NFSR 261) | Poland Junction / AZ-69 near Mayer | ~5.9 miles | Follows Big Bug Creek for ~10 miles; car-drivable dirt; "best surface of all, but very twisty and quite narrow"; watch for cattle |
| **Forest Road 670** | Walker, AZ — cross narrow bridge, turn left onto FR 670 (signed "Big Bug Mesa") | ~5.9 miles from turn | Well-graded dirt; 6.9 miles from Walker to the FR 670 turn |
| **Forest Road 103** | Big Bug Mesa Road (main jeep trail across the mesa) | Varies | Rougher; high-clearance recommended |
| **Via Breezy Pines** | AZ-69 near Mayer northeast | Connects through Breezy Pine Rd | Alternative northern approach |
| **Via Senator Highway** | Prescott | FR 261 east | Western approach from Senator Hwy |

**What's at Five Corners:**
- Elevation: **~6,926 ft** (Trail 706 starts here; East Summit of Big Bug Mesa at 6,908 ft is 0.5 mi further)
- **Old corral** — traditional staging/parking area; serves as the main trailhead
- Junction of NFSR 103, NFSR 670, NFSR 261/CR 58, and at least two other forest roads
- Trailhead for **Big Bug Mesa Trail #9434** — hikers here explore a 3×2 mile plateau covered in Ponderosa pine, juniper, and ancient alligator juniper
- **Views:** northeast toward AZ-69 and Dewey-Humboldt; Mount Davis and Mount Union visible to the west
- Wildlife: old mining camp with military vehicles visible on the mesa; spring boxes with rainwater; mule deer habitat (GMU 20A)
- **Big Bug Mesa hike from here:** ~12.83 miles, ~890 ft elevation gain, ~4 hrs 50 min; uses FR 103 plus unmaintained jeep trails and mild cross-country terrain
- Water: Grapevine Springs (reliable, 1+ gal/min), Seaton Spring (often dry), Coyote Spring (usually dry)

### Historic Landmarks Along Poland Road (before Five Corners)

Driving Poland Road from AZ-69 toward Five Corners — the same road residents use from the Goswick Ranch gate — you pass two of the Bradshaw Mountains' most significant mining heritage sites:

**Poland Mine Mill Foundations** (~5 miles up from AZ-69)
- Stone and concrete remnants of Frank Murphy's **20-stamp mill and concentrator** (built 1902)
- Produced ~$750,000 in silver/gold 1900–1912; 1907 alone yielded $130,465 gold + 16,609 oz silver
- Private property; observe from the road

**Poland-Walker Tunnel — Sealed South Portal** (~5–6 miles up)
- The **south (Poland) entrance** to the 8,071-ft tunnel (1.53 miles) that once linked the Poland and Walker mining districts
- Built ~1903 by Frank Murphy; narrow-gauge ore cars hauled by mule through the mountain
- Portal **collapsed in 1949**; now sealed/caved in and visible as a historic landmark
- The north (Walker) end remains open and actively discharges water
- Interior is not passable; water-filled since collapse
- See `04_history_mining.md` for the full tunnel and mill history

**Road closure note:** Big Bug Mesa Road is subject to temporary winter closures (snow/ice); Prescott NF issues notices via [signalsaz.com](https://www.signalsaz.com)

## 2. Lynx Lake Recreation Area

**Distance: ~28 miles** | Elevation: 5,500 ft | 15 min SE of Prescott

**Fishing:** Rainbow trout, largemouth bass, crappie, catfish; 125,000+ visitors/year; regularly stocked; bag limits: 6 trout, 6 bass/day; unlimited crappie/catfish; AZ fishing license required (14+; annual resident: $37). Swimming prohibited.

**Boating:** 10 HP max; kayak/canoe/sail popular; Lynx Lake Store has rentals (late spring–early fall; closed Mondays)

**Hiking:** Lakeshore Loop Trail — 2-mile circuit; mostly accessible; some steep sections

**Camping:**
- Lynx Campground: 39 sites, April–Oct; https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/234762
- Hilltop: 38 sites, ¼ mi south; same rules

**IMPORTANT STATUS NOTE:** Lynx Lake was **intentionally drained in late 2024** for dam valve repairs. The lake was slowly refilling as of March 2026, but the boat ramp remained closed. **Verify current status before visiting:** call Bradshaw Ranger District at (928) 443-8000.

Sources: https://www.prescott.com/lynx-lake | https://www.fs.usda.gov/recarea/prescott/recarea/?recid=75178

## 3. Horsethief Basin Recreation Area

**Distance: ~20–25 miles** | Elevation: ~5,800 ft

**Key fact:** USFS states "the best route from Prescott is by way of Mayer" — this area is accessed *through* Mayer.

**Access:** SR 69 → Antelope Creek Rd → Cordes → FR 259 → Crown King → FR 52 SE ~6 mi

**Trails:** 9 trails totaling ~30 miles; destinations include Horsethief Canyon, Twin Peaks, Castle Creek, Jim Creek

**Fishing:** Horsethief Basin Lake — bluegill, bass, catfish. **Note: fish consumption advisory for largemouth bass from this lake.**

**Camping:** Hazlett Hollow (15 CCC-era sites); Horsethief Cabin (reservable): https://www.recreation.gov/camping/campgrounds/234077

## 4. Crown King Ghost Town

**Distance: ~14 miles** | Elevation: 5,771 ft

**History:** Gold mining began 1875; at peak, 500 buildings and $2 million in gold from Crown King Mine alone. Bradshaw Mountain Railroad served 1904–1926. Crown King high school students commute to Mayer for school.

**Current attractions:**
- **Crown King Saloon** — originally the Oro Belle Saloon (1910); hotel, café, bar; famous for jalapeño hamburgers; center of community life
- General Store / Post Office
- Historic cemetery
- One-room red schoolhouse (built 1917, still operational)
- Abandoned mines and equipment

**Getting there from Mayer:**
- **Senator Highway (scenic):** SR 69 in Mayer → Pine Flat Road west → Senator Highway south ~26 miles. Maintained gravel; heavily washboarded; high-clearance vehicle strongly recommended; 4WD advisable in wet conditions. ~1–1.5 hours. Spectacular scenery.
- **Bumble Bee / I-17 route (more reliable in winter):** I-17 Exit 248; passes Bumble Bee and Cleator ghost towns along the old railroad bed.

## 5. Agua Fria National Monument

**Distance: ~15–20 miles south** | Free; open 24/7/365 | No water on site

*See also `03_geography_environment.md` for more detail.*

| Entry | I-17 Exit | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Badger Springs | 256 | ~1 mi gravel to trailhead; most popular |
| Bloody Basin Rd | 259 | Graded first 5 mi; high-clearance/4WD beyond; accesses Perry Mesa |
| 3M Ranch Rd | 262 | Via Cordes Lakes; accesses Sycamore Mesa |

**Highlights:** Badger Springs Canyon (permanent river with pools and cascades; excellent birding); Perry Mesa Archaeological Complex (Pueblo la Plata, 100+ rooms, AD 1250–1450; petroglyphs). Audubon-designated Important Bird Area.

**Best times:** October–April. **Avoid** June–early September (110°F+).

## 6. Hunting

Goswick Ranch falls within or adjacent to **Game Management Units 20A and 20B**.

| Species | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Mule Deer | Common | All elevations; archery hunting restricted ¼ mi from occupied residences in parts of unit |
| Merriam's Turkey | Stable | Higher Bradshaws; Horsethief Basin; spring shotgun + fall archery |
| Javelina | Common | Rocky washes, prickly pear stands |
| Gambel's Quail | Common | Chaparral foothills |
| Pronghorn | Present | Unit 20A known for trophy pronghorn |
| Black Bear | Present | Distributed through forest |
| Mountain Lion | Present | Dog hunting primarily |
| Elk | Rare/transient | Very low density; limited draw |

- Mourning/White-winged Dove: Early season Sept 1–15, 2025; Late season Nov 21, 2025–Jan 4, 2026; daily bag 15

**Distance: ~30 miles** | Elevation: 6,398 ft

## 10. Water Recreation

### Lakes Within 30 Miles

**Watson Lake (Prescott, ~30–31 mi):** Kayak/canoe rentals; no motorized boats; Audubon IBA; camping April–Oct; Peavine Trail along shoreline.

**Willow Lake (Prescott, ~31 mi):** ~400 acres; no motorized boats; canoeing, kayaking, birding, fishing, camping April–Oct.

**Yavapai Lakes (Prescott Valley, ~22 mi):** 4.3-acre community fishing lake; regularly stocked; no camping, no swimming, no motorized boats.

### Lakes 30–60 Miles

**Lake Pleasant (~40–45 mi south):** 10,000+ acres; full marina (Scorpion Bay + Pleasant Harbor); water skiing, jet skiing, houseboating; best bass fishing in the Phoenix area; 165 campsites; 20 miles of hiking trails; open 365 days.
