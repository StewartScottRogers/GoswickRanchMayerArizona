# Arizona HOA Law — Planned Communities Act

*Last updated: 2026-04-01*
*Research compiled: 2026-03-28 to 2026-03-29*

---

## Overview

**Primary governing statute:** Arizona Revised Statutes Title 33, Chapter 16 — Arizona Planned Communities Act (§§33-1801 through 33-1818)

The Arizona Planned Communities Act applies to all planned communities: developments with real estate managed by a nonprofit entity where all separate owners are mandatory members paying assessments. The Act does **not** apply to timeshare plans, condominiums (governed separately by §§33-1201–1270), or unmanaged developments.

**Goswick Ranch HOA is governed by this Act.** The association is organized as a nonprofit entity; board members have statutory fiduciary obligations to all members.

**Full chapter text (as amended through September 26, 2025):** https://gshoalaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/GS-Planned-Communities-Act-2025-distribution.pdf

### Governing Document Hierarchy

1. Articles of Incorporation (establishes legal status as nonprofit)
2. Declaration / CC&Rs (recorded core restrictions — highest enforceability)
3. Bylaws (operational procedures)
4. Rules and Regulations (day-to-day policies — most flexible)

When documents conflict, documents higher in the hierarchy prevail.

### Key Definitions (§33-1802)

- **Association:** A nonprofit entity created under a declaration to manage planned community portions and assess members to pay costs.
- **Common Expense Lien:** Legal claim covering assessments, late payment charges authorized in the declaration, plus collection costs and court-awarded attorney fees.
- **Community Documents:** The declaration, bylaws, articles of incorporation, and rules governing the development.
- **Planned Community:** A development where the declaration requires separate owners to be mandatory members paying assessments (excludes timeshares and condominiums).

## A.R.S. § 33-1804 — Open Meetings

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01804.htm

### Who Must Open Their Meetings

All meetings of the members' association, the board of directors, and any regularly scheduled committee meetings must be open to all members or any person designated in writing by a member.

### Member Speaking Rights

- Members must be allowed to speak during deliberations
- The board may impose **reasonable time limits**
- Members must be given the opportunity to speak **once after the board has discussed a specific agenda item but before the board takes formal action** on that item

### Notice Requirements

| Meeting Type | Notice Period | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Board of directors meetings | At least **48 hours** in advance | Newsletter, conspicuous posting, or other reasonable means |
| Annual meetings | **10–50 days** in advance | Mail, hand delivery, or electronic (if member consented) |

Agendas must be made available to all members before the meeting, detailing any proposed amendments, assessment changes, or director removals.

### Recording of Meetings

- Members may record any open meeting by audio or video; **the board cannot require advance notice** of recording
- The board may adopt reasonable rules governing how recording is conducted
- **If the board records a meeting**, it must keep a copy for at least **six months** and make the unedited recording available to any member on request (amended 2025 — SB 1039)

### Executive (Closed) Session — Only 5 Permitted Reasons

1. Consultation with legal counsel regarding attorney-client matters
2. Discussion of pending or anticipated litigation
3. Discussion of personal, financial, or health information about specific individuals
4. Discussion of employee performance or compensation issues
5. Hearing of member violation appeals (unless the member requests the session be open)

**Virtual meetings** are permitted under Arizona law with proper procedures.

## A.R.S. § 33-1805 — Association Financial and Other Records

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01805.htm

### Records That Must Be Kept Permanently

- CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions)
- Original articles of incorporation
- Bylaws
- Deeds and easements
- Meeting minutes (all board and member meetings)

### Time-Based Retention

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Fine notices | Duration of ownership + one year after |
| Ballots, envelopes, election materials | At least **one year** after the election |
| Meeting recordings (if recorded) | At least **six months** (2025 requirement) |

### Member Access Rights

- Members may examine records at **no charge**
- Association must provide copies within **10 business days** of written request
- Copying fee: up to **$0.15 per page** — association cannot charge to view documents in person

### Records That May Be Withheld

- Attorney-client communications
- Pending or anticipated litigation details
- Personal, health, or financial information about individual members, employees, or contractors
- Employee performance and compensation records
- Minutes of closed executive sessions

### Documents That Should Be Available (Best Practice / Statute-Aligned)

Per §33-1805 and §33-1806, HOA websites and member portals should provide:
1. CC&Rs (Declaration)
2. Bylaws
3. Rules and Regulations
4. Current operating budget
5. Most recent annual financial report or summary
6. Most recent reserve study (if one exists)
7. Board meeting minutes (open sessions only)
8. Board member contact information
9. Assessment amounts

### Website Requirement — Arizona Status

**Arizona does NOT currently have a statutory requirement for HOAs to maintain a public-facing website.** The disclosure obligations under §33-1805 require that records be "reasonably available for examination" upon written request — which can be satisfied by allowing in-person review.

*Note: Some HOA industry articles reference a January 1, 2025 requirement for associations with 100+ units to maintain a website with a resident portal. This refers to California law (AB 1458), NOT Arizona law.*

**Practical implication:** Building a website is best practice and streamlines compliance with §33-1805 — it is not legally mandated.

## A.R.S. § 33-1806 — Resale Disclosure Requirements

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01806.htm

When a member notifies the association in writing of a pending sale, the association must deliver all required disclosures within **10 business days** of receiving written notice.

### What Must Be Disclosed

**Governance documents:**
- Copy of the CC&Rs (declaration)
- Copy of the bylaws
- Copy of the rules

**Dated statement containing:**
- Principal contact information for the association
- Current common assessment amounts
- Any unpaid assessments on the unit
- Insurance coverage status for the unit
- Total reserve funds held by the association
- Any documented alterations or improvements that violate the declaration (association need not look back more than six years)
- Pending litigation details involving the unit
- A **purchaser acknowledgment statement** that the CC&Rs, bylaws, and rules constitute a contract between the association and purchaser, and that the association may foreclose if assessments are not paid — this statement requires a purchaser signature and must be returned to the association within **14 calendar days**

**Financial documents:**
- Current operating budget
- Most recent annual financial report (a summary is acceptable if it exceeds 10 pages)
- Most recent reserve study, if any exists

**Litigation summary:**
- Pending lawsuits naming the association (excluding member assessment collection actions)

### Fees the Association May Charge

| Fee Type | Maximum |
|---|---|
| Base fee | **$400 aggregate** |
| Rush fee (72-hour delivery) | **$100 additional** |
| Update fee (30+ days after original) | **$50** |

Fees are collected only at close of escrow; charged once per transaction. Violation penalty for overcharging: up to **$1,200 civil penalty**.

## A.R.S. § 33-1803 — Assessments and Late Fees

- Late fees capped at **$15 or 10% of the unpaid amount**, whichever is greater
- Associations cannot increase regular dues more than **20% annually** without member majority approval

## A.R.S. § 33-1807 — Assessment Liens and Foreclosure

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01807.htm

### Common Expense Lien

- Recording the declaration itself constitutes **record notice and perfection** of the common expense lien — no further recordation required
- Lien covers assessments, late charges, collection costs, and court-awarded attorney fees
- **Priority:** Common expense liens rank above most encumbrances except pre-declaration liens, recorded first mortgages, and real property tax liens

### Pre-Foreclosure Requirements

Before filing a foreclosure action, the board must:
1. Provide **written notice** at least **30 days** before turning the account over to collections
2. **Exercise reasonable efforts to communicate with the member and offer a reasonable payment plan**

### Foreclosure Threshold (Updated 2025 — SB 1494)

| Threshold | Pre-2025 | Post-2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Delinquency period | 12 months | **18 months** |
| Dollar amount | $1,200 | **$10,000** |

Foreclosure may proceed when **either** threshold is met (whichever occurs first). Foreclosure must be **judicial** (not non-judicial).

### Statute of Limitations

Common expense lien is extinguished unless proceedings are instituted within **6 years** after the full amount of the assessment becomes due.

### Payment Application Order

All payments apply first to: (1) assessments (oldest first), (2) late fees, (3) collection costs, (4) attorney fees.

### Member (Non-Lien) Expenses

Member expenses (fees, charges, penalties that are NOT common expense liens) require a separate civil judgment and cannot be foreclosed.

## A.R.S. § 33-1808 — Flag and Sign Rights

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01808.htm

### Flags That Cannot Be Prohibited

Associations **cannot prohibit** outdoor display of:
- The American flag (displayed per the federal flag code)
- Official or replica flags of the uniformed services
- POW/MIA flag
- Arizona state flag
- Arizona Indian nations flags
- Gadsden flag
- First responder flags (including combined designs)
- Blue and gold star service flags
- Historic American flag versions, including the Betsy Ross design

**Associations MAY regulate (but not ban):**
- Limit to two wall-mounted flagpole holders
- Restrict display to no more than two flags simultaneously
- Cap flagpole height at no more than rooftop height

### Political Signs

**2025 amendment:** Flags were added to the definition of "political sign" under both §33-1261 and §33-1808 (effective September 26, 2025).

- **Timing restrictions:** Associations cannot prohibit political signs outside the window from **71 days before** the primary election through **15 days after** the general election
- **Size/quantity:** Where no local limits exist, aggregate dimensions may not be restricted below **9 square feet total**
- **Content:** Associations cannot dictate which candidates or ballot measures are supported, nor require commercially produced signs
- **HOA election signs:** Members may display signs regarding board candidates and community ballot measures from ballot distribution through **3 days after** the election

### Other Signage Rights

- **Caution signs for children:** Permitted if professionally manufactured, under 3 feet tall, displayed only when children are present within 50 feet
- **For sale/rent/lease signs:** Cannot be restricted; must comply with "industry standard size" (not exceeding 18" × 24"); if an association violates this restriction, it forfeits its lien rights for **6 consecutive months**
- **Door-to-door political activity:** Cannot be prohibited except between sunset and sunrise
- **Peaceful assembly in common areas** for community discussions cannot be unreasonably restricted

## A.R.S. § 33-1816 — Solar Energy Devices

- Associations **cannot prohibit** solar energy device installation or use
- Reasonable **placement restrictions** are permitted (but cannot effectively prevent use)

## A.R.S. § 33-1809 — Parking

HOAs with pre-2015 declarations may no longer enforce parking restrictions on **public roads** unless membership votes to maintain this authority by June 30, 2025.

*(Note: Goswick Ranch's internal roads are private — this section applies primarily to public road enforcement.)*

## A.R.S. § 33-1812 and § 33-1813 — Elections and Removal

**§33-1812:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01812.htm
**§33-1813:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01813.htm

### Annual Meeting Requirements

- HOAs must hold an annual meeting **at least once per year**
- Notice: not fewer than **10 days** nor more than **50 days** in advance
- Business conducted: director elections, officer/committee reports, budget approval, assessment votes, document amendments, member input

### Proxy Voting

**Proxies are prohibited** after the termination of the period of declarant control. No one may cast votes on behalf of another owner. Absentee ballots are permitted, but not proxies.

### Voting Methods

- Associations **must** provide for votes cast in person and by absentee ballot
- Associations **may** additionally allow electronic delivery (email, fax)
- Electronic ballots must protect voter secrecy; association must use a secure system to confirm voter identity

### Absentee Ballot Requirements

- Must set forth each proposed action clearly with opportunity to vote for or against each item
- Valid for **one specified election or meeting only** (expires automatically after)
- Must include a deadline at least **7 days** after distribution
- Must state: "The ballot does not authorize another person to cast votes"
- Must include voter identification (name, address, signature) except in secret-ballot communities
- Absentee and remotely-delivered ballots count toward quorum

### Quorum

Default quorum: **10% of eligible voters** if governing documents don't specify otherwise. Can be satisfied through in-person attendance or mail-in ballots.

### Ballot Integrity

- Appoint independent **inspectors of election** (not candidates, officers, or management company representatives) to tabulate votes
- Retain ballots, envelopes, and all related materials for **at least one year** after the election
- Establish a nominating committee at least **75 days** in advance of the annual meeting

### Removal of Board Members (§33-1813)

- Members may remove board directors by **majority vote** at a meeting, with or without cause (except declarant-appointed members)

**Petition thresholds:**

| HOA Size | Required Signatures |
|---|---|
| ≤1,000 members | 25% of votes OR minimum **100 signatures** |
| >1,000 members | 10% of votes OR minimum **1,000 signatures** |

- Board must schedule the removal meeting within **30 days** after receipt of petition
- If board fails to schedule the meeting, all directors are automatically removed
- Quorum for removal meeting: at least **20%** of eligible voters (or minimum 1,000, whichever is less)
- Removed directors cannot serve again until their original term expires

## A.R.S. § 33-1817 — Architectural Review Committee (ARC)

**Statute:** https://www.azleg.gov/ars/33/01817.htm

### Committee Composition (Required by Law)

- **At least one member of the board of directors must serve** on the ARC
- That board member **must serve as chairperson**
- Regularly scheduled ARC meetings must be **open to association members**

### Approval Standard

"Approval of a construction project's architectural designs, plans and amendments shall **not unreasonably be withheld.**" Decisions must apply policies consistently and without bias.

### Response Times

Many CC&Rs provide that if the ARC fails to approve or deny a submission within **30 days**, the submission is **automatically approved**. Verify Goswick Ranch's specific CC&Rs for the applicable deadline. The timeline only begins once all required documents have been submitted.

### Standard ARC Application Contents

- Owner name, address, lot number, contact information
- Written description of proposed work
- Plans, drawings, or specifications appropriate to scope
- Site plan showing setbacks
- Materials list and color samples
- Proposed start and completion dates
- Signature of property owner
- Evidence of required building permits (if applicable)

### Rejection and Appeals

- Rejection letters must be written and must reference the specific policies violated
- Must include appeal procedures if available
- Keep accurate notes and records of each decision

### New Construction Security Deposits (§33-1817)

When associations require security deposits for residential construction:
- Trust account management costs split **equally** between association and member
- A final design approval meeting must occur with member attendance opportunity
- Association must conduct **at least two formal on-site reviews** during construction
- Written reports of any deficiencies must be provided within **5 business days** of each review
- Within 30 days following the second review, if no deficiencies are found, the association must promptly release the deposit

## 2025 Arizona HOA Legislative Updates

**Effective date for most bills:** September 26, 2025
**Primary source:** https://gshoalaw.com/az-hoa-legislative-update-2025/
**Also see:** https://www.azhoalaw.net/article/2025-legislative-update

### Bills Signed Into Law (Effective September 26, 2025)

**SB 1039 — Meeting Recording Retention**
If an association records any board meeting or annual/special meeting of the members, it must keep the unedited recording for at least **6 months** and make it available to any member on request. The board cannot require advance notice that a member intends to record.

**SB 1494 — Foreclosure Threshold Increase**
Raises both thresholds before foreclosure may proceed: delinquency period from 12 months → **18 months**; dollar amount from $1,200 → **$10,000**. Either threshold, when met first, allows foreclosure to proceed.

**SB 1337 — Electronic Violation Notice Response**
Members may now respond to violation notices electronically.

**Political Signs Amendment (§33-1261 and §33-1808)**
Adds flags to the definition of "political sign" for both condominiums and planned communities, effective September 26, 2025.

**Small Claims Jurisdiction (§22-503)**
Jurisdictional limit increased from $3,500 → **$5,000**, making small claims a more practical venue for minor HOA disputes.

**Construction Hours Law (§9-500.52 and §11-269.30) — Effective May 13, 2025**
Counties cannot prohibit general construction activities during these hours:
- May 1–October 15: weekdays **5:00 AM–7:00 PM**; Saturdays **7:00 AM–7:00 PM**
- Concrete pouring permitted at least one hour before general construction activities begin

**Accessory Dwelling Units (ADU) Clarifications**
Clarifies ADU regulations while explicitly protecting private CC&Rs. Owner-occupancy may be required for short-term rentals in units with certificates issued after September 14, 2024.

### Bills That Did Not Pass (Were Tracked in 2025)

- HB 2095: Three-fourths member approval for declaration amendments
- HB 2279: Virtual meetings and absentee ballot authorization; proxy prohibition
- HB 2442: Budget-based assessments; inflation cap requiring member ratification for increases above inflation
- HB 2410: Statutory fiduciary duties for board members
- HB 2656: Reduce declarant control from 4 years to 2 years

## Self-Managed HOA Guidance

### Board Fiduciary Duties

Every board member in Arizona has a **fiduciary duty** to all members. Three core components:

1. **Duty of Care:** Board members must make informed decisions before acting or voting. This requires research before fining homeowners, making financial commitments, or changing rules.

2. **Duty of Loyalty:** Board members must act fairly, in good faith, and in the interest of the HOA as a whole — not for personal benefit. Conflicts of interest must be disclosed; conflicted board members must recuse.

3. **Duty to Act Within Scope of Authority:** Board members must ensure their actions comply with the association's governing documents and Arizona state law.

### Board Officer Roles

| Role | Primary Responsibilities |
|---|---|
| **President** | Presides over meetings; handles legal matters; signs contracts; ensures parliamentary procedure |
| **Vice President** | Assumes president's duties when absent; may oversee specific committees |
| **Secretary** | Records meeting minutes; maintains official records and governing documents; handles member notices |
| **Treasurer** | Maintains financial records; reviews financials monthly; oversees annual operating budget; manages dues collection |

### Financial Requirements

- **Annual financial audit:** Required at end of every fiscal year (§33-1810); audit information must be made available to owners within **180 days** of fiscal year end
- Budget must be based on "reasonable and good faith estimate" of expenses

### Insurance Requirements for Self-Managed HOAs

**General Liability Insurance:**
Protects against accidents and incidents in common areas (roads, gates, common facilities). Essential coverage for any HOA with common areas.

**Directors and Officers (D&O) Insurance:**
Protects board members from personal liability arising from governance decisions. Covers lawsuits claiming breach of fiduciary duty. Most policies do NOT cover willful negligence, fraud, or intentional breach of governing documents. Strongly recommended for all HOA boards.

**Property and Casualty Insurance:**
Covers physical structures owned by the HOA (signs, gates, common area improvements). Especially important in Arizona due to monsoon and wildfire risks.

### Key Self-Managed HOA Best Practices

1. Develop and follow a formal document retention policy
2. Establish a nominating committee at least 75 days before annual meeting; distribute candidate bios; use independent inspectors of election
3. Record and retain meeting minutes permanently; approve at subsequent meeting
4. Review vendor contracts for conflicts of interest (§33-1811); conflicted board members must disclose and recuse
5. Document all violations; apply rules uniformly to avoid selective enforcement claims
6. Track Arizona HOA law changes through the Community Associations Institute (CAI) or HOA law resources

## Dispute Resolution

- The **Arizona Department of Real Estate** administers the HOA Dispute Process via administrative hearings (no court required)
- **Key contacts:** AZ Dept. of Real Estate, AZ Attorney General, AZ Corporation Commission (nonprofit filings)
- ADRE HOA disputes: https://azre.gov/hoa
- Homeowners Protection Bureau: https://www.hopb.co/arizona
- Small claims court: up to $5,000 (as of 2025); practical venue for minor HOA disputes
