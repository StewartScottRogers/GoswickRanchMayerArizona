# Nearest Community & Local Services — Mayer, Arizona

*Last updated: 2026-04-01*

---

## Role in the Goswick Ranch Context

**Mayer, AZ is the nearest town to Goswick Ranch** — approximately 1 mile from the Poland Road / AZ-69 junction. It is a small rural community on SR 69, providing gas, groceries, hardware, a post office, basic medical, and a library. Residents needing larger stores (Walmart, Costco, Target, hospital) drive ~13 miles south to Prescott Valley or ~27 miles southwest to Prescott.

Mayer is not the defining identity of Goswick Ranch. The Ranch's geographic anchor is **Poland Road** and the **Bradshaw Mountains** — Mayer simply happens to be the closest incorporated community at the base of that road.

## History

### Founding
- Original Yavapai name: **"Wi:kidoʼyoʼ"**
- Founded **1882** by **Joseph Hoffmayer** (went by Joseph Mayer), a gold prospector and entrepreneur from New York
- His wife **Sarah Belle Wilbur** became the town's first postmistress in 1884
- Mayer granted railroad right-of-way access; **Southern Pacific Railroad began service in 1898**
- A brickyard and Mayer Business Block (1902) with saloon, barber shop, and general store were established
- Mayer funded two schools — one in town, one for miners' children
- Joseph Mayer died November 28, 1909 (accidental self-inflicted gunshot); "the largest funeral ever held in this city"

### Key Historical Events

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1880s | Gold and silver mining boom in the Big Bug District |
| 1882 | Town founded |
| 1890 | Original community largely destroyed by a dam collapse |
| 1898 | Southern Pacific Railroad arrives |
| 1917 | Mayer Smokestack constructed (129.5 feet tall, built to carry smelter smoke 8 miles from the Henrietta Mine) |
| 1942 | **Mayer Assembly Center** — one of 17 temporary WWII Japanese American detention camps; held 245 people (69 families) before transfer to Poston |
| 2017 | **Goodwin Fire** (28,516 acres) + subsequent Big Bug Creek flood: 109 homes damaged (see `07_emergency_preparedness.md`) |

### National Register of Historic Places (3 sites)
1. Mayer Apartments
2. Mayer Business Block
3. Mayer Red Brick Schoolhouse
(Also notable: Mayer Market, Hotel, Smokestack)
