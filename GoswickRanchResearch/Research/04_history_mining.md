# History & Mining — Big Bug District & Henrietta Mine

*Last updated: 2026-04-01*

---

## Overview

The land that Goswick Ranch occupies sits within one of Arizona's historic gold and silver mining districts. The Big Bug Mining District in the Bradshaw Mountains produced approximately **627,000 ounces of gold between 1867 and 1959**, and the Henrietta Mine — which lies within or immediately adjacent to the subdivision — is among the district's most significant and longest-active mines. Understanding this history is valuable context for the HOA website's "About the Area" content, and the mine's current active status has real-world implications for well water quality and air quality.

Street names inside the subdivision reflect this heritage: **S Miners Pick Rd** and **E Elizabeth Mine Rd** are named for the area's mining history.

---

## Big Bug Mining District

- **Location:** Bradshaw Mountains, Yavapai County, Arizona — directly encompasses the Goswick Ranch area
- **Gold production:** ~627,000 oz (1867–1959); ~42,700 oz from placers
- **Other mines in the district:** Blue Bell, Iron King (between Humboldt and Mayer; $100M total production before 1968 closure), Boggs, French Lily, Little Jessie, Silver Belt
- **Known hazard:** The Big Bug district is a known source of **acid mine drainage and metal leaching** risk to groundwater — one of the reasons arsenic testing of private wells is important at Goswick Ranch (see `01_goswick_ranch_hoa.md`)

### History Timeline

**Pre-1866** — Operating as "Big Bug Mine"
- ~50 ft deep by 1866; producing surface ore
- 1866: among the more active mines in the district

**1883–1884** — "The most prominent mine in the district"
- At peak of early production era

**~1890–1900** — Rediscovery and renaming
- Property apparently renamed for **Henrietta Crossman**, a popular actress and entertainer of the era (exact circumstances unclear; common practice of the period to name mines for well-known figures)

**1915–1919 — Big Ledge Copper Company (Major Redevelopment)**
- The most significant development era
- Construction of a **2,200-ft tunnel** — the main horizontal access drive
- Connected to the **Mayer Smokestack** — a 129.5-ft smelter stack constructed in the town of Mayer in 1917 to carry smelter smoke 8 miles away from the mine workings
- Operations shut down in 1918 as World War I ended and copper prices collapsed

**1923 — Huron Copper Mining Company**
- Reorganized from Big Ledge era
- Ore shipments documented in 1926 and 1930

**1984–1986 — Poland Junction Mining Corp. (Mayer, AZ)**
- Constructed a gravity mill
- Produced approximately **$1.2 million in gold** during this period

**2019** — Henrietta Mine LLC incorporated (Chandler, AZ)

**2020** — New lode claim HM-2 (16.07 acres) staked; 18 active claims total

**April 2022** — ADEQ opened public comment period for **Air Quality Permit No. 92038** for crushing and screening operations at the mine
- ADEQ notice: https://azdeq.gov/notices/comment-period-begins-new-air-quality-permit-crushing-and-screening-henrietta-mine-yavapai

**June 29, 2023** — Arizona State Mine Inspector held a **reclamation public meeting** for Henrietta Mine LLC
- ASMI info: https://asmi.az.gov/meetings/reclamation-public-meetings-operator-henrietta-mine-llc

### Current Status (as of 2026)

- **Owner:** Henrietta Mine LLC (Chandler/Mesa, AZ) — 18 active claims
- **Land status:** Private property
- **ADEQ air quality permit (2022):** For crushing and screening operations — suggests active or proposed mineral processing
- **Arizona State Mine Inspector reclamation proceedings (2023):** Indicates ongoing regulatory oversight
- **Assessment:** The mine appears to be in an active or near-active state, not fully abandoned

---

### Implications for Goswick Ranch

1. **Well water quality:** The mine's proximity is one reason why arsenic and heavy metal testing of private wells is important. Acid mine drainage from the Big Bug district is a documented source of metal leaching (see `01_goswick_ranch_hoa.md` for details).

2. **Air quality:** The 2022 ADEQ air quality permit application for crushing and screening suggests mineral processing activity. Residents should monitor ADEQ notices for updates.

3. **Property values:** The Google Earth aerial overlay shows lower-value adjacent parcels (~$43K–$60K) near the mine tailings vs. the Goswick Ranch premium ($250K–$750K range), likely reflecting proximity to mine operations.

4. **Historical character:** The mining heritage explains the subdivision's street naming (S Miners Pick Rd, E Elizabeth Mine Rd) and is part of the area's authentic Western history. It can be presented as character, not liability — with appropriate well-water awareness.

## Mayer Smokestack — Local Landmark

The **Mayer Smokestack** (constructed 1917) was built at 129.5 feet tall in the town of Mayer itself to carry smelter smoke 8 miles away from the Henrietta Mine's Big Ledge Copper Company operations. It is one of Mayer's most notable historical features, reflecting the scale of early 20th-century mining operations in the Big Bug District.
