## Zoning: RCU-2A Details

| Provision | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| RCU = | Residential / Rural Community Unincorporated |
| County minimum | 2 acres (RCU-2A designation) |
| Goswick Ranch minimum | **9 acres (CC&R restriction — private covenant)** |
| Livestock | Permitted; subject to Allowed Animal Chart (Sec. 501E, Yavapai Co. Ordinance) |
| Horses | Explicitly permitted; equestrian community |
| Camping | Permitted on 2-acre+ lots; 10 days at a time; 3x/year; 30 days between stays |
| Commercial uses | Not permitted in RCU |
| ADUs | One attached + one detached permitted (Yavapai Co. adopted Nov 2025; max 1,000 sq ft); CC&Rs may further restrict |
| Short-term rentals | State law allows in unincorporated areas; CC&Rs may further restrict |

### Open Range Law — Important for Goswick Ranch

Arizona is a **"fence out" (open range) state.** Property owners who do not want livestock on their land must build a legally compliant fence ("lawful fence"):
- Posts spaced no more than 30 feet apart
- At least 4 strands of barbed wire
- Wire heights: 50", 38", 28", and 18" above ground

**Critical point:** If you hit and kill or injure livestock on the open range, you (the driver) are liable to the livestock owner, not the reverse. Yavapai County is open range outside incorporated municipalities.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Mayer median property tax | ~$476/year |
| 25th percentile | ~$257/year |
| 90th percentile | ~$1,362/year |
| National median | ~$2,400/year |
| Yavapai County property tax rate | **0.44%** — well below national average (0.89%) |
| Assessment cycle | Spring (notices by mid-March) |

**For a $300,000 Goswick Ranch property:** ~$1,320/year = $110/month — dramatically lower than comparable properties in Scottsdale, Phoenix suburbs, or even Prescott proper.

**Senior Property Tax Freeze:** Homeowners age 65+ with qualifying income (~$46,400 single / ~$58,000 joint) can freeze assessed value for 3 years (renewable). Significant protection in an appreciating market.

**Agricultural Classification (Class 2):**
- Primary use must be agricultural with reasonable expectation of operating profit
- 9-acre lots generally do not qualify for cropland (20 ac minimum) or orchard (10 ac minimum) classifications
- Exception: high-density or equine operations have no minimum acreage — a genuine commercial equine training/breeding/rescue operation *might* qualify
- Hobby farms, personal gardens, and private livestock do NOT qualify
