### `03_geography_environment.md`

**What it contains:** The physical and ecological character of Goswick Ranch's location — the Bradshaw Mountains, Poland Road corridor, Big Bug Creek, native plants, wildlife species lists, dark skies, monsoon season, water quality, and Agua Fria National Monument.

**Key facts and data:**
- Bradshaws: 40×25 mile north-south range; highest peak Mt. Union 7,979 ft
- Poland Road (E Poland Rd / CR 58 / NFSR 261): runs from AZ-69 to Five Corners (6,926 ft), ~5.9 miles; car-drivable dirt
- Big Bug Creek: perennial-to-seasonal stream; USGS gauge site 09507980 (linked on HOA website); FEMA Zone AE
- Vegetation zone: Interior chaparral transitioning to montane conifer at ~5,500–6,000 ft
- Key trees: ponderosa pine, alligator juniper, Emory oak, Arizona white oak, pinyon pine
- Key shrubs: pointleaf manzanita, shrub live oak, mountain-mahogany
- Saguaro NOT present at 6,000 ft (upper limit ~4,500–5,000 ft)
- Wildlife: Mule deer, black bear, mountain lion, javelina, coyote, wild turkey; rattlesnakes (black-tailed, Arizona black) active April–October
- Birds: Golden Eagle, Broad-tailed Hummingbird (primary breeding species), Acorn Woodpecker, Steller's Jay, Mexican Spotted Owl
- Dark skies: Bortle Class 3–4; Milky Way visible; best viewing Oct–Nov and Jan–May
- Arsenic: 43.6% of Yavapai County wells exceed EPA 10 ppb; mining district leaching risk
- Monsoon: Official season June 15–Sept 30; peak July–August; flash flood risk elevated after any upstream fire
- Agua Fria National Monument: 71,000 acres, ~15–20 miles south; free; I-17 exits 256/259/262; petroglyphs, birding, dispersed camping
- Geology: Precambrian granite and gneiss (~1.7–1.8 billion years old); "Bradshaw Granite"; highly mineralized

### `04_history_mining.md`

**What it contains:** The mining heritage of the Goswick Ranch area — the Big Bug Mining District, the Poland Mine and Poland Road landmarks, the Henrietta Mine (a currently active operation within or adjacent to the subdivision), and the Mayer Smokestack.

**Key facts and data:**
- Big Bug Mining District: ~627,000 oz gold produced 1867–1959
- Poland Road named for D.R. Poland (1834–1882), who discovered the Poland Mine in 1872 and sold it for ~$3.5 million (modern equivalent) in 1879
- Poland-Walker Tunnel: 8,071 ft long (1.53 miles); built ~1903 by Frank Murphy; south portal visible from Poland Road (collapsed 1949); north portal still discharges water
- Poland Mine: 20-stamp mill built 1902; $130,465 gold + 16,609 oz silver in 1907 alone
- Street names reflect heritage: S Miners Pick Rd, E Elizabeth Mine Rd
- **Henrietta Mine (USGS MRDS #10027183):** Located within or immediately adjacent to Goswick Ranch
  - Also known as: Henrietta-Gopher Mine, Big Bug Mine, American Flag Mine, Braganza Gold Mine
  - 18 active claims; 2,200-ft tunnel; ~7,000 ft total workings; 1,000 ft maximum depth
  - Owner: Henrietta Mine LLC (Chandler/Mesa, AZ)
  - ADEQ air quality permit (2022) for crushing and screening — suggests active operations
  - Arizona State Mine Inspector reclamation proceedings (June 2023)
  - Primary commodity: Gold; secondary: copper, silver, lead
  - Assay: 3.2% Cu, 0.2 oz Au/ton, 2.7 oz Ag/ton
  - 1984–1986: produced ~$1.2M in gold
  - Aerial image in `Documents/GoswickRanchGoogleEarth.png` shows tailings pile visible in the community
- Mayer Smokestack: 129.5 ft tall; built 1917 to carry Henrietta Mine smelter smoke 8 miles; still stands in Mayer

**Implications for the website:**
- Well water: Mine proximity is why arsenic and heavy-metal testing matters
- Air quality: 2022 ADEQ permit means residents should monitor ADEQ notices
- Property values: Lower-priced adjacent parcels (~$43K–$60K) near mine tailings vs. Goswick Ranch premium ($250K–$750K)
- Framing: Mining heritage can be presented as historical character; street names are a natural reference

**When to use:**
- History section of the About page
- Any disclosure-related content (arsenic warning, well testing FAQ)
- Outdoors page (Poland Road landmarks as a day-trip feature)
- Any time a resident asks about the mine visible from aerial photos

### `10_arizona_hoa_law.md`

**What it contains:** Complete coverage of the Arizona Planned Communities Act (ARS §§33-1801 through 33-1818), including all key sections with statute text summaries, 2025 legislative updates (three bills signed), self-managed HOA operational guidance, board officer roles, insurance requirements, and dispute resolution options.

**Key facts and data:**
- Governing statute: ARS §§33-1801–1818; full 2025 text at gshoalaw.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/GS-Planned-Communities-Act-2025-distribution.pdf
- **§33-1804 (Open Meetings):** All meetings open to members; 48-hr notice for board meetings; 10–50 days for annual meetings; members may record without advance notice; only 5 reasons for closed session
- **§33-1805 (Records):** CC&Rs, bylaws, and meeting minutes kept permanently; copies within 10 business days of written request; max $0.15/page copying fee
- **§33-1806 (Resale Disclosure):** Within 10 business days of written notice of sale; disclose CC&Rs, bylaws, rules, budget, reserve study, assessments, litigation; base fee max $400; rush fee (72-hr) max $100 additional
- **§33-1807 (Liens/Foreclosure):** 2025 update — foreclosure threshold raised to 18 months OR $10,000 (whichever first); must be judicial, not non-judicial
- **§33-1808 (Flags/Signs):** Cannot prohibit US flag, AZ flag, service flags, Gadsden flag, first responder flags, Betsy Ross design; political signs protected 71 days before primary through 15 days after general
- **§33-1817 (ARC):** At least one board member must serve as ARC chairperson; ARC meetings open to members; approval cannot be unreasonably withheld; auto-approval if no response within 30 days (verify in CC&Rs)
- **2025 Bills (effective September 26, 2025):** SB 1039 (recording retention ≥6 months), SB 1494 (foreclosure threshold increase), SB 1337 (electronic violation notice response), small claims jurisdiction raised to $5,000
- Document hierarchy: Articles of Incorporation → CC&Rs → Bylaws → Rules & Regulations
- **Arizona does NOT require HOAs to maintain a public website** (unlike CA, FL, TX, NV); building one is best practice, not a legal mandate
- Late fees: capped at $15 or 10% of unpaid amount; dues increases >20%/yr require member majority
- Annual financial audit required (§33-1810); results available to owners within 180 days of fiscal year end
- ADRE HOA dispute process: https://azre.gov/hoa

**When to use:**
- Building or updating the ARC page (§33-1817 is the primary statute)
- Building or updating the Documents page (§33-1805 disclosure obligations)
- Building or updating the For Buyers page (§33-1806 resale disclosure)
- FAQ answers about HOA authority, owner rights, elections, board removal
- Any time the board asks about what they're legally required to do
- **Verify current statute text at azleg.gov before publishing anything** — statutes can be amended

### `12_ranch_lifestyle_governance.md`

**What it contains:** The lifestyle and cultural context for living at Goswick Ranch — what draws people to rural Arizona ranch living, horse culture in Yavapai County, RCU-2A land use rules (livestock, ADUs, STRs, camping), agricultural tax classification, open range law, equestrian services, the four seasons at elevation, and detailed profiles of Prescott, Prescott Valley, Verde Valley, Jerome, Sedona, Phoenix Metro access, and Arcosanti.

**Key facts and data:**
- Chickens: max 8 per lot in RCU; roosters prohibited in residential zones
- ADUs (adopted Nov 2025): one attached + one detached permitted; max 1,000 sq ft; 5-ft side/rear setbacks; CC&Rs may still restrict
- STRs: State law allows in unincorporated areas; events (weddings, parties) prohibited; CC&Rs may further restrict — verify with board
- Camping on property: 10 days at a time, 3x/year, 30 days between stays
- Agricultural tax classification: 9-acre lots generally do NOT qualify for standard cropland (20 ac min) or orchard (10 ac min); equine operations (commercial, profit-motivated) have no minimum acreage
- Assessment ratio: Agricultural Class 2 is 15% (higher than residential Class 3 at 10%) — agricultural classification rarely benefits small properties
- **Open range law:** Arizona is "fence out" state; if you hit livestock on the road you are liable, not the livestock owner; lawful fence = 4 strands barbed wire at 50/38/28/18 inches; posts ≤30 ft apart
- Los Caballos Vet: (928) 636-6644; serves Mayer; 24/7 emergency
- Olsen's Grain (Dewey/Humboldt, ~15 mi): (928) 632-5233; livestock feed, hay, delivery
- Four seasons table: Winter (40–50s°F, occasional snow), Spring (wildflowers Feb–Apr), Summer (80s°F, monsoon), Fall (oak/cottonwood color, hunting season)
- Monsoon: Arizona sees 500,000+ lightning strikes per season; petrichor from desert rain; half the year's precipitation in 8 weeks
- Prescott (27 mi): Whiskey Row, Courthouse Plaza, World's Oldest Rodeo (since 1888), Sharlot Hall Museum ($9), Phippen Western Art Museum ($12), Prescott Airport (United Express to LAX/DEN daily)
- Prescott Valley (13 mi): YRMC East, Walmart/Costco/Target, NextCare Urgent Care
- Verde Valley Wine Trail: AVA designated 2021; Page Springs Cellars, Alcantara, Caduceus (Maynard James Keenan)
- Jerome (~40 mi): National Historic Landmark; Jerome Grand Hotel (haunted); ghost tours
- Sedona (~60 mi): Red Rock Pass $5/day; arrive by 8am on weekends; Cathedral Rock (1.5 mi moderate)
- Sky Harbor (PHX): 74 miles, ~70 min
- Arcosanti (~11–15 mi): Paolo Soleri's arcology experiment; tours Thu–Mon 9:30am/11am/2pm; bronze windbells
- Distance table: Prescott 27 mi, YRMC East 13 mi, Sky Harbor 74 mi, Arcosanti 11–15 mi

**When to use:**
- Building or updating the Living Here page (livestock rules, four seasons)
- Building or updating the Outdoor Recreation / Day Trips page (Prescott, Verde Valley, Sedona, Arcosanti)
- For Buyers page (lifestyle context, equestrian services)
